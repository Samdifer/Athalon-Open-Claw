## §3. DUE_SOON Item Review

### Item D-1: Main Rotor Head Assembly (Yoke) — 189.6 hours remaining

**ALS Item:** Main Rotor Hub Assembly (Yoke), P/N 76250-00200
**Reference:** Sikorsky S-76C ICA §04.2.1
**Life limit:** 4,000 flight hours from manufacture (total time)
**Aircraft TT (at review):** 3,810.4 hours
**Hours remaining:** 189.6 hours
**Previous status (Phase 29 WS29-C):** DUE_SOON at 3,726.4 hr (273.6 hr remaining)
**Status at review:** ⚠️ DUE_SOON — URGENT

**Significance:** At Lone Star Rotorcraft's current utilization rate (N76LS averages approximately 25 charter hours per week across the Fort Worth corporate market), 189.6 hours represents approximately **7.5 weeks** from the review date. The Main Rotor Hub (Yoke) is a Part 29 life-limited component — its retirement is mandatory at 4,000 hours total time, not subject to deferral or engineering disposition. When N76LS reaches 4,000 hours, this component must be removed from service.

Replacement of the Main Rotor Hub Yoke is not a line-maintenance operation. The part requires removal of the main rotor head assembly, which requires a Sikorsky-authorized or Part 145 repair station with helicopter capability and tooling (Sikorsky S-76C toolset, rotor head stand, torque wrench calibrated to S-76C ICA §04.2.1 specifications). The work is estimated at 12–16 hours of labor. The replacement part (P/N 76250-00200 or approved alternate) must be on order in advance — helicopter rotor head components are not stocked locally; lead times from Sikorsky parts distribution are typically 4–8 weeks.

**Sandra:** "I know. I've been watching this one since the data entry session. At the current pace we'll hit 4,000 hours in mid-June. The part has to be ordered now. I've already called Sikorsky Support. They quoted me 6 weeks delivery on P/N 76250-00200 — that means I need to order it today if I want it here before the aircraft runs out of time."

**Tobias:** "The work itself isn't difficult — I've done rotor head teardowns on this airframe twice. But we'll need to schedule a full-day slot to do it right. Tear-down, replacement, reassembly, main rotor track and balance before RTS. Plan for two days."

**Marcus:** "This is the canonical example of what ALS tracking is for. Without the 4,000-hour limit on the dashboard, this conversation happens when N76LS is at 3,990 hours and the part is on back-order. With the dashboard, it happens now — when Sandra has time to order the part, book Tobias's schedule, and plan the downtime without a crisis."

## §6. Product Lessons — Feedback to Athelon Team

Sandra's debrief and the DUE_SOON scheduling session generated two product insights:

**Lesson 1: Lead time awareness**
Sandra's comment about the 6-week lead time for rotor head components points to a product gap: Athelon currently shows hours remaining on an ALS item but doesn't show how that compares to typical parts lead times. A future feature (F-1.4-E backlog, noted) could allow DOMs to enter a "procurement lead time" for known long-lead components, and the system could surface a "Order parts now" alert when remaining hours drops below lead time threshold.

**Marcus note:** "This is the right product direction. The 189-hour window for the S-76C main rotor hub is only meaningful if you know the part has a 6-week lead time. Without that context, 189 hours sounds like planning horizon. With it, it sounds like urgency. Devraj should capture this in the product backlog."

**Lesson 2: Combined maintenance optimization**
Tobias's suggestion to combine all three retirement events in one June window is a planning insight that the system didn't surface. Athelon currently shows each DUE_SOON item independently. There's an opportunity for a "maintenance event clustering" view — grouping items by projected due window — that would help DOMs plan combined maintenance events more efficiently.

**Devraj (noted from session notes):** "Added both to F-backlog: F-1.4-E (procurement lead time awareness) and F-1.4-F (maintenance event clustering). Neither is in v1.4 scope but both are informed by real operator use."
