## 3. Switching / Dissatisfaction Evidence

### Shops Switching TO EBIS (churn signals for competitors)

| Signal | Company | Detail | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Explicit switch** | Basin Aviation | "In the process of moving away from another solution" — prior software unnamed; switched to EBIS. Basin is a small FBO/MRO. | Competitor lost a customer; EBIS is gaining small-shop wins |
| **Explicit switch** | Qmulus Aviation | "The existing limitations of the previous system hindered Qmulus Aviation's ability to scale." Previous system unnamed. After acquisition by Sprintbach Aviation, switched to EBIS. | EBIS winning post-acquisition transitions |
| **Explicit switch** | Plane Place Aviation | "The existing software... fell short in supporting their growing business model" — missing professional forms, reporting, labor tracking. Switched to EBIS. | EBIS positioning as upgrade for growth-stage shops |

### EBIS Friction Points (potential Athelon sales angles)

| Source | Issue | Quote |
|---|---|---|
| Capterra (reviewer) | Complex configuration | "The system has so many configuration options, it can be a bit overwhelming to setup initially." Recommends relying on EBIS implementation team. |
| Capterra/SoftwareAdvice snippet | Fit for size | "Better fit for a smaller maintenance operation. The price point was also more in line for a smaller maintenance shop." — suggests EBIS self-selects smaller shops, limiting upmarket potential |

### Corridor Friction Points

| Source | Issue | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| G2 / Slashdot listings | General review listings exist but no specific negative reviews surfaced in search | No named shops found publicly expressing dissatisfaction with Corridor |
| Market positioning | Corridor targets larger MROs / enterprise accounts (Jet Aviation, GAMA Aviation, ExecuJet scale) | May leave mid-market underserved — Athelon opportunity |

### No Explicit Corridor Churns Found
No public evidence of shops announcing they left Corridor or replaced it with another system. Corridor's customer list is heavily enterprise-weighted (Jet Aviation, GAMA, Wheels Up, ExecuJet) which may indicate stickiness at the enterprise level.

### No Named "EBIS Alternative" Searches Yielded Shop-Level Signals
Searches for "EBIS alternative aviation" and "corridor alternative aviation" returned only software comparison sites (Capterra, Slashdot, SoftwareSuggest), not shop-level commentary. No aviation forum posts or LinkedIn discussions about switching from either platform were surfaced.

## Research Notes & Caveats

1. **EBIS claims 300+ shops** on platform (as of 2021 cloud relaunch). The case studies represent a small, publicly visible sample.
2. **Corridor claims 500+ customers** (third-party listing site). Named case studies and testimonials are confirmed; the customer logos page contains higher-confidence identifications.
3. **ExecuJet MRO Services** appears associated with Corridor (primary evidence: video interview) but EBIS has a company sub-page for them. Classify as Corridor pending further investigation.
4. **GAMA Aviation and Jet Aviation** are global enterprise MROs that could use multiple systems across locations. Their presence on Corridor's customer logos page suggests at least some use.
5. **Alaska Airlines EBIS usage is GSE-only** (ground support equipment), not aircraft MRO — likely not an Athelon competitor touchpoint.
6. The Capterra comparison searches show EBIS commonly compared against: Flightdocs, iFlight MRO, Cirro, SMS Pro — indicating which alternatives EBIS customers evaluate.
