## What Athelon Is

Athelon is maintenance tracking software for your shop. It replaces the parts of Corridor you use every day: work orders, task cards, step sign-offs, maintenance records, and the return-to-service process. It runs in a web browser — your office computer, a tablet in the hangar, your phone. Everything updates in real time. When Troy signs off a step on the floor, you see it on your screen immediately.

The records Athelon produces are designed to survive an FAA inspection without you in the room to explain them. Every signature is a real authentication event — not a text field. Every record is permanent — no one can go back and silently edit a signed entry. Every work order has a complete audit trail showing who did what and when.

---

## What You'll Be Able to Do in the Pilot Session

We'll walk through a 100-hour inspection on N1234A (Cessna 172) from start to finish:

1. **Create and open a work order.** You'll enter the aircraft total time from the logbook. The system checks that it's consistent with what's on file.
2. **Troy works through the task card.** Five inspection steps. He signs each one with a PIN — the system verifies it's really him at the moment of signing. Step 5 requires an IA holder, so Pat signs that one.
3. **Troy creates the maintenance record.** He documents the work performed, references the C172 maintenance manual, and notes the torque wrench he used (the system shows whether its calibration is current).
4. **You review the Close Readiness Report.** One screen. One checklist. Green or red for each item — task cards, maintenance records, AD compliance, aircraft time. If anything is red, the system tells you exactly what and takes you directly to it.
5. **You authorize return to service.** You enter your PIN. The system verifies all nine preconditions are met. N1234A flips to Airworthy. The work order closes.
6. **Linda performs the QCM review.** After you close the work order, Linda reviews it inside the system — not in a spreadsheet. Her review is signed and audited just like every other record.

## What We're Asking You to Test

We don't need you to find bugs. We need you to tell us whether this feels right.

Specifically:
- **Before you sign anything**, you'll see a summary of exactly what you're signing — aircraft, work order, your certificate number. Does it show you what you need to see?
- **The close readiness screen** — is it the "one screen, one checklist" you described to us? Do you trust it?
- **The signing process** — PIN entry, 2-second confirmation delay, signature recorded. Does it feel like a real signature event, or does it feel like clicking a button?
- **The audit trail** — after the work order closes, can you find who signed what and when? Does it take less than two minutes?

We'll also ask Linda the same kind of questions about the QCM review.

---

## What to Do If Something Feels Wrong

Say so. Out loud, to Nadia. That's her job during this session — to listen to you.

If the system shows you something that doesn't match what actually happened — wrong aircraft, wrong time, wrong person — tell us immediately. We stop.

If something is confusing but not wrong — a label that doesn't make sense, a button in the wrong place, a screen that needs scrolling when it shouldn't — tell us that too. We write it down.

If you're not sure whether something is wrong or just unfamiliar — say that. "I'm not sure about this" is the most useful thing you can tell us.

---

## What Your Feedback Will Be Used For

Everything you and Linda tell us during this session goes directly into what we build next. Your exact words. Not our interpretation of your words.

If you say the close readiness screen needs a sixth check we didn't think of — we add it. If you say the signing process takes too long — we fix it. If you say this is close but you wouldn't use it for a real aircraft yet — we need to know why, and we'll come back when it's fixed.

This is the first time a real mechanic at a real shop uses this software. What you experience today determines whether Athelon becomes something your shop actually uses, or something that looked good in a demo and went nowhere.

We built this for people like you. Now we need you to tell us if we got it right.
