## Block 1: Core Sales Funnel Model (Hormozi's Constraint Analysis Case Study)

From S1 (sqlpatterns.com):

**Funnel Stages:**
```
Show Up → Make Offer → Close Sale → Revenue
```
Tracked as conversion rates:
- **Show Rate** = people who show up / people registered for appointment
  - Benchmark: ~70% (Hormozi case study found 49% = constraint)
- **Offer Rate** = people who are presented the offer / people who showed
  - Should be near 100% if show rate is healthy
- **Close Rate** = people who buy / people who were made an offer
  - Benchmark: ~40% (case study found 27% = constraint)
- **Total Sold** = North Star / Goal metric

**Constraint-finding methodology:**
1. Map all funnel stages
2. Assign benchmark targets (from industry pattern recognition)
3. Find which stage is most off target AND most upstream
4. Fix organizational issues BEFORE fixing conversion issues
5. Org structure is the root constraint in sales functions

**Metrics Tree concept:**
- Yellow boxes = conversion rates
- Blue boxes = absolute numbers
- Red boxes = input levers (things you can manipulate to impact conversion rates)
- Each stage's output feeds into the next

## Block 2: KPI Benchmarks & Acquisition.com Pattern Recognition

From S2 (Dickie Bush $45k workshop):

- Acquisition.com collects full business metrics 2 months before advisory sessions
- They pattern-match submitted metrics against their portfolio to identify THE constraint
- Benchmark comparison is key diagnostic tool — they know "what good looks like" from many businesses
- KPIs that were "within benchmark": show rate, close rate, offer rate for the Dickie Bush business
- They ONLY fix the constraint — other areas get no attention until constraint is resolved (Theory of Constraints)
- Weekly iteration cadence: "deploy weekly tests on whichever stage in the funnel could see 50% improvement"
- 2-5% improvement per week compounds dramatically over 18 months

**Setter/Closer Structure observed:**
- 4 closers : 3 setters ratio
- "Free for all" lead routing instead of round robin
- Rationale: rewards proactive setters, most important factor for throughput
- Setters: outbound qualification + appointment booking + closer edification
- Closers: inbound consultative close using CLOSER framework

## Block 3: Sales Call Methodology — CLOSER Framework

From S4, S5:

**C — Clarify:** Why are we here? Uncover needs, wants, pain, desire.
- "What made you take this call?"
- "What's your goal right now?"
- "Why is that important to you?"

**L — Label:** Name the problem. Non-threatening recap.
- "So it sounds like XX is your goal"
- "Does that sound about right?"

**O — Overview:** Pain history. What have they tried before that didn't work?
- "What have you tried so far?"
- "How did that work for you?"
- "Why do you think that didn't work?"

**S — Sell the Vacation:** Pitch. Identify top 3 result-drivers. Sell destination not journey.

**E — Explain:** Handle objections. Three objection archetypes:
1. Blaming circumstances
2. Blaming others
3. Blaming self
- Solution: Build trust + product belief + surface money access

**R — Reinforce:** Post-sale emotional confirmation. Videos, cards, swag, onboarding. "People decide within 48hrs of sale whether they'll buy again."

## Block 4: Pre-Sales & Qualification System

From S5 (Naveen's Hormozi notes):

**Qualification Framework: BANT**
- Budget: Can they afford it?
- Authority: Are they the decision-maker?
- Need: Do they genuinely need it?
- Timing: Is now right?
- Re-confirm BANT on closing call even if qualified earlier

**Setter's Pre-Call Duties:**
1. Edify the closer ("Sarah helped 30 businesses like yours achieve X")
2. Qualify lead using BANT
3. Record notes in CRM for closer to reference
4. Book appointment with specific time slots

**Closer's Pre-Call Duties:**
1. 5-minute pre-call research (website, social, CRM notes)
2. Reference setter notes at call open: "Hey, [Name], [Setter] was telling me you're X, Y, Z..."
3. Set agenda immediately — respect time, show control

**Inbound lead script (Hormozi style):**
> "[Name]? This is [Your Name]. I'm calling because you opted in on Facebook for our promotion — I wanted to make sure you weren't a crazy person on the internet [laughs] and find a time that works for you."

**Outbound curiosity script:**
> "Hey [Business Name], I'm calling about [Competitor]. Give me a call back."

## Block 5: Compensation Structure

From S3 (learnbizfaster.com / Hormozi YouTube):

**Tiered Commission by Conversion Rate:**

| Close Rate Threshold | Outcome |
|---|---|
| <50% | Terminated (lose job) |
| 50% | Keep job (baseline) |
| 60% | Recognition / small perks |
| 70% | Commission: 10% → 15% (50% raise) |
| 80%+ | Commission: 25% |

**Philosophy:**
- "Winners win — losers lose" — intentionally unequal distribution
- Not a flat rate — "ratchet" system means comp adjusts to performance
- Top performers make significantly more than average
- Natural attrition of bottom performers is by design, not accident
- Comp structure is the first filtering mechanism

**Implication for CRM:**
- Must calculate individual rep close rates in real time
- CRM must support tier assignment and commission calculation by period
- Comp changes should be automatic and visible to the rep

## Block 6: Manager Cadence & Coaching

From S6 (wellthatmakessense.com — Hormozi Part 2 synthesis):

**Manager Role:**
- Stability, generosity, strong work ethic — lead by example
- Active engagement: regular feedback, role-plays, coaching sessions
- Culture carrier — not just metric enforcer

**Performance Environment Tools:**
- Visual leaderboards (public, visible, real-time or near-real-time)
- KPI scorecards per rep
- Regular sales competitions (can be tied to short-term incentives)

**Pruning:**
- Cut bottom 10% periodically to maintain culture of excellence
- This is a *forcing function* for the rest of the team
- The team must understand high standards are non-negotiable

**Training System (from S7 — Hormozi quote):**
> "On someone's first day, I let them know I'll probably stop them 30 or 40 times as they go through the script. That's actually a good sign — it means we're improving."
- High-frequency feedback = compressed learning
- Script mastery before live calls (modeled → role-played → shadowed → solo)
- Role-play cadence: ongoing, not just onboarding

## Block 7: CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization) as Operating Cadence

From S1, S2:

**Weekly Test-and-Iterate:**
1. Identify which funnel stage is furthest from benchmark
2. Design one targeted intervention (script change, follow-up sequence, etc.)
3. Deploy and measure for one week
4. Compare before/after conversion at that stage
5. Repeat — find next constraint

**Constraint-first principle:**
- Do NOT optimize non-constraint stages — waste of effort
- Every week ask: "Which stage is the weakest link?"
- Upstream problems always have bigger leverage than downstream

**Key insight from S1:**
> "Org structure represents the biggest constraint, especially in a sales function. If your sales people aren't managed well, no amount of effort in fixing lead quality or conversion rates will matter."
