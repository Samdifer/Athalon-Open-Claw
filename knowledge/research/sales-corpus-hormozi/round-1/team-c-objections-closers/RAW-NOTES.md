## SECTION A: OBJECTION TAXONOMY (Hormozi Lens)

### A.1 Core Insight — What Objections Actually Are
- Hormozi's root claim: Prospects **want to buy**. They wouldn't be there otherwise.
- Objections = expressed fear, not genuine rejection
- The three underlying fears driving all objections:
  1. **Fear of making a mistake** (wasting money, being wrong)
  2. **Fear of looking dumb** (peers, family, boss judging them)
  3. **Fear of status loss** (if this doesn't work, what does that say about me?)
- They want to trust you, but have often been burned before
- Implication: the rep's job is not to "overcome" the objection but to **dissolve the fear beneath it**

### A.2 Hormozi Objection Categories (synthesized from sources)

#### PRICE OBJECTIONS
- "It's too expensive"
- "I can't afford it"
- "I need a lower price"
- Root fear: Fear of wasting money / making a financial mistake
- Hormozi reframe logic: The cost of NOT solving the problem is greater than the price.
  > "If your reason is 'I can't afford this program,' that's the very reason you should do it. How much longer do you want not being able to afford something to be the thing that holds you back?" (Source-008)
- Underlying tactic: Shift the comparison from "price vs. zero" to "price vs. cost of the status quo"
- Related: Anchoring to the dream outcome's value, not the product's sticker price

#### TRUST / SKEPTICISM OBJECTIONS
- "How do I know this will work?"
- "I've tried things like this before"
- "What certifications/proof do you have?"
- Root fear: Past failures (circle of pain) — been burned before
- Hormozi tactic: The Overview step — **intentionally excavate past failures** to surface this fear, then use it to increase contrast with your solution
- AAA Application: Associate the trust question with smart buyer behavior: "That's exactly the question our best clients ask before they commit..."
- Straw man technique: "I had another client who asked the same thing. Here's what I told them..."

#### TIMING OBJECTIONS
- "Not the right time"
- "Let me think about it"
- "Come back to me in 6 months"
- Root fear: Fear of commitment, fear of being wrong, fear of making a permanent decision
- Hormozi framing: Timing objections are almost never really about time — they're about unresolved concerns
- Response approach (AAA): "Totally understand, we covered a lot today and this is a big decision. What would make this a no-brainer for you?"
  - This moves from "timing" to the real objection hidden beneath
- Additional tactic: Surface the cost of delay — "What does another 6 months of [the problem] cost you?"

#### AUTHORITY / APPROVAL OBJECTIONS
- "I need to talk to my spouse/partner"
- "I need to run this by my boss"
- "I can't make this decision alone"
- Root fear: Fear of accountability — wants someone else to share the decision weight
- Hormozi framing: "Coach your champion" — if they must talk to someone, arm them with the answer to the objection their gatekeeper will raise
  - "Before you do, what's the one thing you think they'll push back on?" (Source-002)
- This reveals the underlying objection before the meeting happens and lets the rep pre-handle it
- Do NOT say: "Great, let me know what they say." That is ceding control.

#### FIT / QUALIFICATION OBJECTIONS
- "I'm not sure this is right for me"
- "Is this for someone in my situation?"
- "I don't think I need all of this"
- Root fear: Fear of buying something irrelevant, fear of embarrassment if they're wrong
- Hormozi approach: The Label step — help them see exactly which phase of the journey they're in and how the product maps to that phase
- Reframe: Their doubt about fit shows they're being discerning — reframe that as a mark of a serious buyer, then address the specific concern

## SECTION B: FRAMING AND REFRAME TECHNIQUES

### B.1 New Opportunity vs. Improvement Framing
- Key Hormozi principle: **New opportunity beats improvement offer**
- Why:
  - Improvement = reminds prospect of past failure → triggers shame/resistance
  - New opportunity = no baggage, no prior failed attempts, no commodity pricing
- Sell: A fresh vehicle for reaching the destination, not a "better" version of something they've already tried and failed at
- Implication for objections: When a trust/past-failure objection arises, pivot to the novelty of the approach

### B.2 Value Equation as Reframe Tool
**Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**
- Price objection reframe: Increase the numerator (make them feel the dream is bigger, more achievable)
- Timing objection reframe: Reduce Time Delay in their mind (show how fast results come)
- Trust objection reframe: Increase Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (proof, guarantees, case studies)
- Fit objection reframe: Connect their specific dream outcome to your vehicle

### B.3 The CLOSER Framework — Full Step Detail
**C — Clarify (Who is here and why?)**
- Don't accept "just looking" — everything that brought them to this conversation is evidence of a problem
- Probe: "What pain points, challenges, or problems led you to book this call today?"
- If deflected: "(light tone) You wouldn't spend your valuable time speaking with me unless something was going on — what really brought you here?"

**L — Label (Identify and agree on the root problem)**
- Don't move forward until both parties agree on THE problem
- Use: "Tell me more about that..." / "Of everything you mentioned, what's the #1 obstacle to [outcome]?"
- Segment the problem by the prospect's stage: early-stage vs. scaling vs. transition
- Goal: Make the prospect feel **understood before they feel sold to**

**O — Overview (Circle of Pain)**
- Excavate past failures: "What have you tried before?" / "What didn't work about that?"
- Why: Surfaces the trust objection early; also increases felt contrast with your solution
- Capture both positives and negatives from past attempts — integrate liked elements into your pitch
- For high-ticket: Stop here, create engagement hook (pre-call survey) to increase commitment before the close call

**S — Sell (Vacation, not the flight)**
- Hormozi's phrase: "Sell the vacation, not the flight"
- Describe the outcome state vividly — beaches, not boarding gates
- Don't explain features/logistics yet — make them **feel** where they're going
- Bridge: Recap C-L-O before stepping into the sell ("Based on what you shared, here's what I see for you...")

**E — Explain (Handle resistance preemptively)**
- Before asking for the sale, preempt objections: "Some people at this stage wonder about [X]..."
- This is where objection scripts live — surface and dissolve resistance before it becomes a block
- Don't rush to close immediately after pitching — give space for concerns
- Apply AAA for any objection that surfaces

**R — Reinforce (Post-close)**
- After a yes: reinforce the decision immediately
- Personalized follow-up to prevent buyer's remorse
- Frame: "You made the right call. Here's what happens next..."
- Reactivate the dream outcome they described in Step 1

### B.4 The Triple A (AAA) Framework — Full Detail
**Step 1: ACKNOWLEDGE**
- Repeat the objection back in a neutral, understanding tone
- "So you're saying [paraphrase]..."
- Why: Demonstrates active listening; builds rapport; buys processing time
- Critical: Don't parrot robotically — use warm, genuine delivery

**Step 2: ASSOCIATE**
- Link the objection behavior to behavior of successful buyers
- "That actually tells me you're taking this seriously — which is exactly what our best clients do before they commit"
- "That's a great question, it shows you're being thoughtful about this"
- Why: Transforms prospect from defensive to validated; removes shame from hesitating
- Variant — Authority Association: Reference a high-authority figure: "Alex just told me this..." / "Our best customer who [big result] asked the same thing"

**Step 3: ASK**
- After reframing, ask a question that takes control and reveals the real concern
- "Which specific part of this feels uncertain to you?"
- "What would need to be true for this to be an easy yes?"
- "What would make this a no-brainer for you?"
- Why: The person asking questions controls the conversation; this prevents the rep from going on defense

### B.5 Straw Man Technique
- Use when delivering a hard truth that could feel confrontational
- Format: "I had a client ask the exact same thing earlier today. Can I share what I told them?"
- Then deliver the truth via the "other client" without making the current prospect feel attacked
- Why: Depersonalizes the correction; makes the prospect feel they're not uniquely confused

## SECTION C: CLOSING STRATEGY PATTERNS

### C.1 The Closing Philosophy
- Hormozi does not believe in "overcoming" objections through brute force
- Closing is the natural endpoint of a well-run discovery → diagnosis → prescription sequence
- If the CLOSER framework is followed correctly, resistance at close should be low
- Primary pre-close failure modes:
  1. Rep didn't clarify the real reason for the meeting
  2. Rep didn't gain agreement on the problem (skipped Label)
  3. Rep pitched features, not outcomes (skipped vacation sell)
  4. Rep didn't surface and dissolve objections before asking (rushed to close)

### C.2 Ask Patterns at Close
- Direct ask: "So, do you want to move forward?"
- Assumptive: "When do you want to get started?"
- Binary: "Does this feel like the right fit for where you're trying to go?"
- Trial close: "If [X concern] were resolved, would you move forward today?"
- Post-reframe ask: Always ask again after applying AAA — don't leave without a second attempt

### C.3 Persistence vs. Pressure
- Hormozi distinguishes between **persistent asking** (acceptable) and **pressure tactics** (unethical)
- Maintaining rapport enables persistent asking without burning the relationship
- "No" is treated as "not yet" only if rapport is intact and reframing hasn't resolved the objection
- Rep rule: Ask at least twice after a "no" — the first no is often reflexive
- Third "no" after full AAA application = genuine disqualification, not an objection

### C.4 Engagement Hooks for High-Ticket Closes
- For high-ticket sales with multi-touch process, use "engagement hooks" between contacts:
  - Pre-call survey with commitment-building questions
  - Example questions: "Why do you think I can help you?" / "What budget have you allocated?" / "When do you expect results?"
- Why: Creates micro-commitments that increase psychological investment before the close call
- Hormozi team uses weekly close incentives (vs. monthly) to drive end-of-week intensity surges

### C.5 Metrics and Activity Discipline
- "Volume negates luck" — the rep who makes more attempts will close more, period
- Track KPIs at every funnel stage: click → lead → call booked → show rate → offer made → close rate
- Compare to benchmarks — Hormozi team benchmarks across portfolio companies
- Weekly testing cadence: small improvements compound; 2–5% improvements per week stack significantly
- Closer incentives: upfront cash collected as the primary metric (not leads, not calls — cash)

## SECTION D: SALESPERSON MINDSET, HABITS, AND PERFORMANCE DRIVERS

### D.1 Core Mindset Principles (Hormozi)
- **Confidence comes from competence, not affirmations**
  - "You become confident by becoming competent" (Source-005)
  - Reps build confidence through reps, not motivation
- **Discipline beats motivation**
  - Motivation fluctuates. Discipline is the system that fills the gap.
  - "Discipline is doing the boring work"
- **Consistency over peaks**
  - "Most people don't fail because they're not capable; they fail because they're not consistent"
  - Outlier sales months are flukes; consistent activity is the mechanism
- **Fear is directional information**
  - "Fear is a compass. It shows you where to go"
  - The call you avoid making is the one you need to make

### D.2 Activity and Volume as Foundation
- Volume is non-negotiable: high performers make more contacts than peers
- "Volume negates luck" — you cannot rely on talent or a hot market; you must make the numbers
- "Every skill you want is on the other side of reps"
- Implication: a rep who does 3× the activity will out-earn a more talented rep at half the activity

### D.3 Skills as Competitive Advantage
- "Skills are the real unfair advantage"
- "Information isn't power. Applied information is power" — knowing a framework ≠ being able to run it live
- Skill development requires deliberate practice: roleplay, call review, script drilling
- Reps should drill objection responses to reflex-level — no thinking under pressure

### D.4 Rep Coaching Framework (synthesized from Hormozi teaching)
- Identify constraint: What stage of the funnel is the rep losing deals? (Clarify? Label? Handling objections? Asking for the close?)
- Prescribe targeted practice: Don't practice what's already working — drill the weakness
- Record and review calls: Reps who review recordings improve faster
- Weekly incentive structure: Cash collected per week (not month) — drives consistent peak effort
- Pipeline hygiene: No "zombie leads" — qualify hard, move decisively
- Fear index: Track which types of calls/objections reps avoid — that's the coaching priority

### D.5 Pre-Call Preparation Habits
- Know the prospect's likely objection category before the call (based on their source/context)
- Rehearse AAA responses for the top 3–5 objections in their market
- Review past call notes / CRM data — never go in cold on a warm lead
- Set the mental frame: "This person wants to buy. My job is to remove the obstacle."

## SECTION E: POTENTIAL CONTRADICTIONS AND TENSIONS

### E.1 Ethics vs. Effectiveness Tension
- The AAA framework and CLOSER are highly effective at moving people past resistance
- Critical Reddit thread (Source-009) describes Gym Launch as using "aggressively persuasive" tactics with bait-and-switch marketing elements
- Tension: Reframing objections as "smart buyer behavior" can be used to manipulate hesitant people into purchases they shouldn't make
- Hormozi acknowledges this risk and states 5 ethical rules for reframing (Source-002)
- The line between "ethical persuasion" and "manipulation" is context-dependent and requires rep judgment

### E.2 "Wants to Buy" Framing — Applicability Limits
- Hormozi's premise (prospects want to buy) is valid for **warm, inbound leads who opted in**
- Applying this framework to cold outbound prospects or disqualified leads is an overextension
- The framework works best with qualified prospects who have a genuine problem the product solves

### E.3 Script vs. Presence Trade-off
- Heavy scripting (drilling responses) can create robotic delivery that breaks rapport
- Hormozi advocates for internalized frameworks, not word-for-word scripts — but practitioners often implement as rigid scripts
- Reps must balance structure with authenticity
