## SOURCE-001
- **Title:** "What I Learned From Alex Hormozi: Sales Training From the $100 Million Dollar Man"
- **URL:** https://thevectorimpact.com/what-ive-learned-from-alex-hormozi/
- **Source Type:** Editorial article / synthesis of Hormozi's public teaching
- **Retrieved:** 2026-03-07 UTC
- **Key Claims Extracted:**
  - CLOSER Framework steps: Clarify, Label, Overview, Sell, Explain, Reinforce
  - Prospects "want to buy" — fear holds them back (fear of mistake, status loss, looking dumb)
  - "Give, give, give, ask" — trust must be built before the ask
  - New opportunity framing is superior to improvement framing
  - Value equation: Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
  - Salespeople must identify the root problem and get agreement on it before pitching
  - "Clarify" step: don't accept "just looking" — dig deeper
  - "Label" step: segment problem by proximity to desired outcome
  - "Overview" step: circle of pain — collect negative past experiences to increase felt contrast
  - "Sell" step: sell the vacation, not the flight — benefits not features
  - "Explain" step: handle resistance by pre-empting and dissolving objections
  - "Reinforce" step: post-sale reassurance to prevent buyer's remorse
- **Confidence:** HIGH — detailed synthesis from multiple Hormozi sources

## SOURCE-002
- **Title:** "Hormozi's 3 A's Sales Framework" — The Follow Up newsletter
- **URL:** https://www.jointhefollowup.com/p/hormozis-3-a-sales-framework
- **Source Type:** Newsletter / editorial summary of Hormozi podcast teaching
- **Retrieved:** 2026-03-07 UTC
- **Key Claims Extracted:**
  - Triple A (AAA) Framework: Acknowledge → Associate → Ask
  - Acknowledge: mirror/repeat prospect's objection back — shows listening, buys thinking time
  - Associate: link the objection behavior to what successful buyers do — reframe as smart/rational
  - Ask: follow with a question that takes control and reveals the real concern
  - "The person asking questions is in control"
  - Reframing = 1–3 sentences said after any non-yes response
  - "Straw man" technique: reference another client/authority to deliver hard truths without confrontation
  - Five ethical rules: (1) Questions control; (2) no one argues with questions; (3) tell them what their question means; (4) use straw men for tough truths; (5) always be curious
  - Example: "I need to think about it" → "Totally understand, we've covered a lot. What would make this a no-brainer for you?"
- **Confidence:** HIGH — directly attributes to Hormozi's Ep 810 podcast teaching
