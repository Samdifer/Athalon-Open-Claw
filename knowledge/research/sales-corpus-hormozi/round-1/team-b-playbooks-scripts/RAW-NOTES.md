## FRAMEWORK 1: C.L.O.S.E.R. Framework
*Source: Multiple secondaries, consistent across Hormozi YouTube + books*

The primary Hormozi sales call structure. Each letter maps to a phase:

### C — Clarify
- Clarify why the prospect is on the call and what they need help with
- Avoid solving the wrong problem
- Uncover needs, wants, pain points, desires
- **Script questions:**
  - "What made you take this call?"
  - "What's your goal right now?"
  - "Why is that important to you?"

### L — Label
- Identify and label the problem out loud
- Prospect must acknowledge/admit the problem themselves
- Recap in non-threatening way: "So it sounds like [X] is your goal — does that sound about right?"

### O — Overview
- Overview the prospect's history with this problem
- "What have you tried so far to accomplish this?"
- "How did that work for you?"
- "Why do you think that didn't work?"
- Purpose: position yourself as the logical next step by understanding what failed before

### S — Sell the Vacation
- Sell the DESTINATION not the journey
- Identify top 3 outcomes/benefits — explain why each matters to THEM specifically
- Show where they'll be AFTER using your product/service
- Details = work; destination = desire
- Do NOT dive into how it works at this stage

### E — Explain (Objections)
- Prospect objections fall into 3 buckets:
  1. Blaming circumstances ("The economy is bad...")
  2. Blaming others ("My partner won't agree...")
  3. Blaming self ("I don't think I can...")
- Position solution to neutralize all three
- Key mindset: "When people trust you, believe your product will solve their problem the way they want it solved, and have access to money — they buy."
- This phase = comfortable conversation about concerns, not pressure

### R — Reinforce
- Once they pay: reinforce the decision immediately
- Give them something to get excited about: personal videos, handwritten cards, swag, personalized onboarding
- "People decide within the first 48hrs from point of sale whether they will buy again"
- Reinforce = reduce buyer's remorse + build long-term retention

## FRAMEWORK 2: AAA Framework (Objection Handling)
*Source: "The Game" Ep 810, Dec 2024 — primary source*

Used to handle objections in real-time during close phase:

### A — Acknowledge
- Repeat the prospect's objection back in a neutral, understanding tone
- DO NOT immediately counter-argue
- Purpose: (1) builds rapport by showing you listened, (2) buys time to formulate response
- Example: "I hear you — it sounds like the timing feels off right now."

### A — Associate
- Draw a parallel between prospect's concern and a previous successful buyer who had the same concern
- "Actually, our last three clients felt the same way about [X], and what they found was [Y outcome]"
- Optionally cite a mentor/authority: "Even [mentor name] told me this exact concern is usually what separates those who [succeed] from those who [stay stuck]"
- Purpose: let the prospect see themselves in a success story

### A — Ask
- After acknowledging and associating, confidently and persistently re-ask for the sale
- Do NOT ask timidly
- "Based on everything we've talked about, does it make sense to move forward?"
- Maintain momentum — silently wait for answer

## FRAMEWORK 3: Pre-Sales System
*Source: Naveen Notes (Hormozi content derivative)*

### Setter → Closer Pipeline
1. **Setter qualification:** Use BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timing) at booking stage
2. **Edification:** Setter builds up closer before handoff ("You'll speak with [Name], who's helped [X clients] achieve [Y result]")
3. **CRM notes:** Closer reviews setter notes before call
4. **Closer opener using notes:** "Hey [Name], [Setter] was telling me you're [situation]. Is it fair to say that's where you're at and what you're trying to accomplish?"

### BANT Qualification Scorecard Points
- **Budget:** Can they afford the solution? (1 = no, 2 = stretch, 3 = comfortable)
- **Authority:** Are they the decision-maker? (1 = needs approval, 2 = influencer, 3 = sole decision-maker)
- **Need:** Genuine need confirmed? (1 = vague, 2 = acknowledged, 3 = urgent/painful)
- **Timing:** Ready to move? (1 = 6+ months, 2 = 1-3 months, 3 = now)
- **Threshold:** Score ≥8 → proceed to close call / Score <6 → nurture

### Pre-Call Research (5-min rule)
- Check website, LinkedIn, social, CRM notes
- Goal: reference at least one specific detail to show you did homework

### Outbound Intrigue Script
> "Hey [Business Name], I'm calling about [Competitor Name]. Give me a call back."
- Triggers curiosity about what competitor is doing
- Does NOT lead with a pitch

### Inbound Re-Engagement Opener
> "[Prospect Name]? [pause] This is [Your Name]. [pause] I'm calling because you opted in on [platform] for our [promotion], and I just wanted to make sure — one, you weren't a crazy person on the internet [laughs], and two, to make sure you knew we weren't crazy people either. I've got [Time 1] and [Time 2] — which works better for you?"

## FRAMEWORK 4: Call Opening — Proof, Promise, Plan
*Source: Naveen Notes / Hormozi content*

Structure for opening the closing call:
1. **Proof:** "We've helped [X businesses/people] in [their situation] achieve [Y result]..."
2. **Promise:** "...and I'd love to explore if we can do the same for you..."
3. **Plan:** "...On today's call, I'll spend the first [X] minutes understanding your situation, then walk you through how we can help, and we'll see if it makes sense to take a next step together. Sound fair?"

## FRAMEWORK 6: 3-Pillar Pitch ("Sell the Vacation" expanded)
*Source: "The Game" Ep 789/790, Nov 2024*

When pitching, break your solution into 3 pillars:
- Use **third-grade language** — no jargon, no complexity
- Use **relatable analogies** — metaphors that map to everyday experience
- Each pillar = one step/requirement for the prospect to achieve their goal
- Format: "There are really three things that need to happen for you to [get result]: [Pillar 1], [Pillar 2], [Pillar 3]. Here's what I mean by each..."

## FRAMEWORK 7: Ethical Scarcity & Urgency
*Source: $100M Leads (Hormozi), secondary analyses*

### Hormozi's Scarcity Principles
- **Real constraints only:** Scarcity must be based on actual business limitations (inventory, time, capacity)
  - Bad: "Only 5 spots left!" (when you have infinite)
  - Good: "I only have [X] onboarding slots per quarter because [real reason]"
- **Time-limited bonuses:** Add a bonus that expires rather than changing core price — more ethical than fake discount cliffs
- **Enrollment periods:** Set real enrollment windows with real deadlines (e.g., cohort-based programs)

### Hormozi's Urgency Principles
- **Loss aversion framing:** "Every month you wait costs you [specific amount or outcome]"
- **Cost of inaction:** Calculate and make visible what NOT acting costs — not manipulated, just math
- **Seasonal/timing relevance:** "Q4 pricing" or "We're at capacity until [real date]" — must be true
- **Future pacing:** "Imagine where you'll be in 6 months if you start now vs. if you wait another 6 months"

## CLOSING TACTICS (Miscellaneous)
*Source: Naveen Notes / Hormozi content*

### When Prospect Says "No" or Hesitates
Rapid-fire diagnostic questions (use one at a time):
1. "What's your main concern?" — early in call
2. "What are you afraid of having happen?" — deeper concern
3. "What variables are you using to make this decision?" — understand criteria
4. "What would make this a yes for you?" — flip to positive
5. "What would make this a no for you?" — clarify negatives

### When Prospect Says "Yes"
**STOP TALKING.** Move immediately to payment/next step. Every additional word risks re-opening doubt.
> "Perfect. Let's get you set up — do you have [card/method] handy?"

### All-Purpose Close
> "Does that sound like a fit for what you're trying to accomplish?"
> [wait for response]
> "Great — what questions do you have for me before we get going?"

### Detail Deflection (Question the Question)
When prospect asks specifics that could box you in:
> Prospect: "How many days a week do you work with clients?"
> You: "Well, how many days were you looking for?"
- Answers to their actual need, not a blind answer

### Authority Close (for tough details)
> "That's a great question — and it's really one for [specialist/expert] to answer precisely. What I can tell you is [related value statement]."

## FOLLOW-UP CADENCE (Post-Call, No Close)
*Source: Inferred from Hormozi principles on engaged leads + community notes*
*Note: Hormozi does not publish a single canonical "follow-up script" — cadence below synthesized from his principles*

### Hormozi's Follow-Up Principles
1. Speed matters — follow up within minutes of any form completion or opt-in
2. "Engaged leads" are the goal — not just contacts (from $100M Leads)
3. Persistent follow-up is professional, not pushy, if you genuinely believe your offer helps them
4. Give value in every touch (resource, insight, case study) — don't just "check in"

### Inferred Cadence (post-call, no close)
| Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-----|---------|---------|
| Same day | Text/call | Same-day summary: "Great talking with you. Here's what I mentioned: [link/resource]. Let me know if you have questions." |
| Day 2 | Email | Case study or testimonial relevant to their specific situation |
| Day 5 | Text | Check-in: "Any questions come up as you thought it over?" |
| Day 10 | Email | New value: insight, tool, resource relevant to their problem |
| Day 21 | Call | Direct: "I want to make sure we figure out if this is a fit — do you have 10 minutes?" |
| Day 30 | Email | Long-term nurture: relevant update, success story |

### No-Show Re-Engagement Script
> "[Name] — you had an appointment with us today at [time]. No worries if something came up. I've got [Time 1] and [Time 2] open this week. Which works better?"
- Frame as offering a reschedule, not confronting the no-show
- Follow up 3x max before removing from active sequence

## ANTI-PATTERNS NOTED
- "Think about it" acceptance → prospect ghosts, sale dies
- Over-explaining after YES → creates doubt, unravels close
- Answering detail questions without knowing their need first
- Fake scarcity / manufactured urgency → kills trust permanently
- Pushing past a hard no without diagnosing real concern
- Starting call without agenda setting → rambling kills authority
- Skipping post-sale reinforcement → buyer's remorse → chargeback/churn
