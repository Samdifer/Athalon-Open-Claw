## Overview

Alex Hormozi's sales system is built around three core books/source bodies:
- **$100M Offers** (2021) — offer architecture, value positioning
- **$100M Leads** (2023) — lead generation, engagement, pre-qualification
- **"The Game" Podcast** (ongoing) — tactical call frameworks, closing scripts, objection handling

The frameworks below follow the sales pipeline stage order: **Pre-Call → Opening → Discovery → Pitch → Close → Follow-Up**.

## TEMPLATE 1: Call Agenda Template
**Pipeline Stage: Call Opening (first 90 seconds)**
**Source: Proof-Promise-Plan framework + ANOT opener (Ep 810, Naveen Notes)**

```
CALL AGENDA SCRIPT — Opening Block

[Use name with upward inflection]
"[Prospect Name]? [pause] This is [Your Name] from [Company]."

[ANOT Frame]
"A few quick things before we get started:
  - I really appreciate you making time today.
  - Naturally, you'll have questions for me — I'll answer everything I can.
  - Obviously, I'll have a few for you too, to make sure I understand where you're at.
  - Typically, I use the first [10 minutes] to get a sense of your situation,
    then I'll walk you through what we do and how it might help,
    and we'll figure out together if it makes sense to take a next step.
  
  Does that sound fair?"

[Proof-Promise-Plan warm-up — optional for cold/unfamiliar prospects]
"Just so you have some context: we've helped [X clients / type of business]
 [achieve specific result] — [Proof].
 My goal for today is to see if we can do something similar for you — [Promise].
 To get there, I want to understand your current situation first — [Plan].
 Make sense?"
```

**Do NOT:**
- Skip the agenda → you lose control of the call
- Over-explain your company's history at this stage
- Ask "how are you doing?" and wait for small talk in B2B contexts

## TEMPLATE 2: Qualification Scorecard (BANT-Based)
**Pipeline Stage: Pre-Call / Setter Stage / First 10 Minutes of Discovery**
**Source: BANT framework referenced by Hormozi (Naveen Notes)**

```
QUALIFICATION SCORECARD

Prospect: _____________________________ Date: _________ Rep: ___________

[Score each 1–3]

BUDGET
  - 1: Cannot afford / unknown budget
  - 2: Can stretch, may need payment plan
  - 3: Budget confirmed / comfortable
  Score: ___

AUTHORITY
  - 1: Needs approval from others (partner, board, spouse)
  - 2: Influencer, not final decision-maker
  - 3: Sole decision-maker, can say yes today
  Score: ___

NEED
  - 1: Vague interest, no clear pain
  - 2: Acknowledges problem, not urgent
  - 3: Urgent, painful, actively seeking solution
  Score: ___

TIMING
  - 1: "Maybe someday" / 6+ months
  - 2: 1–3 months out
  - 3: Ready now or within 30 days
  Score: ___

TOTAL: ___ / 12

ROUTING:
  10–12: Priority close — move to discovery + close same call if possible
  7–9:   Nurture — book follow-up call, send case study, re-qualify in 7–14 days
  <7:    Disqualify or long-term nurture — do not invest close-call time
```

**Anti-patterns:**
- Assuming all booked appointments are sales-ready → wastes closer time
- Re-qualifying on the close call in a way that feels interrogative → break rapport
- Accepting "yeah we have budget" without confirming who signs off

## TEMPLATE 3: Discovery Flow Script (C.L.O.S.E.R. Phases C + L + O)
**Pipeline Stage: Discovery (mid-call, after agenda)**
**Source: CLOSER Framework — Hormozi YouTube, $100M Offers ecosystem**

```
DISCOVERY FLOW — CLOSER (C + L + O)

=== C: CLARIFY ===
"Before we get into anything, I want to make sure I understand exactly 
why you reached out / took this call. In your own words — what's going on 
and what are you trying to accomplish?"

[Listen. Do not interrupt. Take notes.]

Follow-ups:
"Why is that important to you right now?"
"What would achieving that mean for your [business/life]?"

=== L: LABEL ===
"So if I'm hearing you right — [restate their goal in their words].
Does that sound right?"

[Let them confirm or correct]

"And the problem getting in the way of that is [label the problem]?
Would you say that's accurate?"

Key: They must say "yes" out loud. Their acknowledgment unlocks the next phase.

=== O: OVERVIEW (Pain History) ===
"This is probably not the first time you've thought about solving this, right?
What have you tried before?"

[Listen]

"How did that work out?"
"What do you think got in the way?"
"What specifically was missing from [previous solution]?"

[Repeat back key points]
"So you tried [X], it didn't work because [Y], and what you really need is 
something that [Z]. Is that a fair summary?"
```

**Where this fits in the pipeline:**
- First 15–25 minutes of a discovery or sales call
- Must complete before pitching — pitching without this = talking at them
- Skip to Sell the Vacation only when you have explicit pain + history confirmed

## TEMPLATE 4: Pitch Structure — 3-Pillar Pitch
**Pipeline Stage: Pitch (CLOSER "S" — Sell the Vacation)**
**Source: Hormozi "The Game" Ep 789/790; CLOSER framework**

```
3-PILLAR PITCH SCRIPT BLOCK

"Based on everything you've shared, I think there are really 
three things that need to happen for you to [achieve their stated goal]:

PILLAR 1: [Name it simply]
  "The first is [simple label]. Think of it like [relatable analogy]. 
   What this means for you is [specific outcome in their words]."

PILLAR 2: [Name it simply]
  "The second is [simple label]. Most people skip this step because [common mistake], 
   but what it actually does is [benefit]."

PILLAR 3: [Name it simply]
  "And the third is [simple label]. This is the part that [ties it all together / 
   makes the first two work / accelerates the result]."

"Taken together, that's what gets you from [current state] to [desired state].
Does that make sense so far?"

[Transition to fit]
"Now the question is whether what we do maps to those three things — 
and I think it does. Let me show you how..."
```

**Language rules (from Ep 789/790):**
- Third-grade language: no industry jargon without immediate plain-English translation
- Analogies over explanations: "It's like [everyday thing]" always outperforms technical descriptions
- Stay at destination level — do NOT explain implementation detail at pitch stage
- 3 pillars max — more = overwhelm, fewer = under-justified

**Anti-patterns:**
- Feature-dumping (talking about HOW rather than WHAT and WHY)
- Asking for the sale before prospect says "yes" to the destination vision
- Using your internal terminology ("our proprietary methodology") instead of their language

## TEMPLATE 5: Objection Path Tree (AAA Framework)
**Pipeline Stage: Close (CLOSER "E" — Explain away concerns)**
**Source: "The Game" Ep 810 (AAA Framework); Naveen Notes rapid-fire questions**

```
OBJECTION HANDLING — AAA PATH TREE

[ENTRY: Prospect says NO or raises concern]

STEP 1: DIAGNOSE (before AAA)
  Ask ONE diagnostic question:
  
  Early in call → "What's your main concern?"
  Mid-call → "What are you afraid of having happen?"
  Pre-close → "What variables are you using to make this decision?"
  At close → "What would make this a yes for you?"
              "What would make this a no?"

  [Listen. Identify objection type:]

OBJECTION TYPE A: CIRCUMSTANCE ("The timing isn't right / economy is slow / busy season")
  ↓
  AAA Response:
  - Acknowledge: "I completely understand — timing is real."
  - Associate: "Actually, a lot of our best clients started during [similar situation]. 
    What they found was that [outcome] — and they were glad they didn't wait."
  - Ask: "Does it make sense to at least get you set up now so you're ready when [their window opens]?"

OBJECTION TYPE B: OTHERS ("My partner/spouse/boss needs to approve")
  ↓
  AAA Response:
  - Acknowledge: "That makes sense — this is a decision you want to feel good about together."
  - Associate: "We actually have a short version of this call I can do with both of you. 
    Most couples/partners who do that find it only takes [10 minutes]."
  - Ask: "Would it make sense to get them on a quick call together this week?"
  [Avoid: "Can you make the decision without them?" — disrespectful, burns rapport]

OBJECTION TYPE C: SELF ("I don't know if I can do this / I've tried before and failed")
  ↓
  AAA Response:
  - Acknowledge: "That's actually the most honest thing you could say."
  - Associate: "Most people who get results with us felt exactly that way. 
    What made the difference was [specific mechanism]. 
    [Name] told me the same thing before they [achieved result]."
  - Ask: "Given that, does it make sense to at least try [smallest commitment or entry point]?"

OBJECTION TYPE D: PRICE ("It's too expensive / I can't afford it")
  ↓
  Diagnose first: "Is it that it's not in budget at all, or is it that you're not sure the value is there yet?"
  
  If value gap →
  - Re-anchor to cost of inaction: "What does it cost you every month this problem continues?"
  - Calculate: "[Monthly cost × 6] vs. [program cost] — what makes more sense?"
  - If still stuck: "We do have [payment plan / starter option] — would that work better?"
  
  If true budget constraint →
  - Acknowledge: "Understood — I appreciate you being straight with me."
  - Ask: "Is this a timing issue, or is budget genuinely not available for this right now?"
  - If timing → offer future booking / nurture sequence
  - If hard no → gracefully exit: "Makes sense. Can I check back in [X weeks]?"

HARD NO PATH:
  - Accept the no cleanly
  - "Totally understand. Is there a timeline when it might make more sense to revisit?"
  - Set a specific callback date if possible
  - Do NOT press after a clearly-stated final no — it burns the relationship permanently

WHEN THEY SAY YES:
  → STOP ALL SELLING IMMEDIATELY
  → "Perfect. Let's get you set up."
  → Move to payment / contract / next step
  → Zero additional words about benefits/features
```

## TEMPLATE 6: Ethical Urgency & Scarcity Scripts
**Pipeline Stage: Close (enhancing commitment, not manufacturing pressure)**
**Source: $100M Leads; ampifire.com analysis of Hormozi launch; bookbytes.ai summary**

### Principle: Only use urgency/scarcity that is TRUE
*"The most ethical and effective scarcity strategy is to base it on real limitations within the business."* — $100M Leads

```
SCARCITY SCRIPT (capacity-based):
"I want to be transparent with you — we only take [X] new clients per month 
because [specific real reason: onboarding bandwidth, team size, etc.]. 
Right now we have [X] spots open. I can't guarantee that holds past [date/timeframe]."

URGENCY SCRIPT (time-limited bonus):
"If you get started by [real date], we'll include [specific bonus] at no extra charge. 
After that, that's not something I can offer — it's just not realistic for us to include 
it indefinitely."

COST OF INACTION SCRIPT:
"Let me put this another way. Every month this problem continues, 
it costs you approximately [amount or outcome] in [lost revenue / wasted time / stress].
Over 6 months, that's [6× the number]. 
What you'd invest with us is [price]. 
So the question isn't really 'can I afford this?' — it's 'can I afford not to?'"

FUTURE PACING SCRIPT:
"Imagine it's [6 months from now] and you started today.
Where would you be? Now imagine it's [6 months from now] and you waited.
Where does that put you?"
```

**Hard compliance guardrails:**
- NEVER: "Only 3 spots left!" if that's false → FTC deceptive practices, destroyed trust
- NEVER: Countdown timers that reset → same issue
- NEVER: Price that "goes up tomorrow" unless it genuinely does
- ALWAYS: Real deadlines tied to real operational constraints
- ALWAYS: If you extend a deadline, acknowledge it honestly and explain why

## TEMPLATE 7: Follow-Up Cadence Scripts
**Pipeline Stage: Post-Call Nurture (no close on first call)**
**Source: Synthesized from $100M Leads engagement principles + Hormozi follow-up philosophy**
*Note: Hormozi does not publish a single canonical follow-up script; cadence below inferred from his stated principles on engaged lead nurturing*

```
FOLLOW-UP CADENCE — POST-CALL (NO CLOSE)

DAY 0 (same day, within 2 hours):
Channel: Text or WhatsApp
"Hey [Name] — really enjoyed our call today. Here's the [resource/case study] 
I mentioned: [link]. Let me know if any questions come up."

DAY 2:
Channel: Email
Subject: How [Similar Client/Business Type] [achieved result]
"[Name] — thought this might be relevant given what you shared about [their specific situation]. 
[2-3 sentence story of similar client outcome]. 
Worth a quick chat to explore if something similar could work for you?"

DAY 5:
Channel: Text
"[Name] — checking in. Any questions come up as you thought it over?"
[Short. No pitch. Just open door.]

DAY 10:
Channel: Email
Subject: [Value-add: insight, tool, resource]
"[Name] — saw this and thought of you. [1-paragraph relevant insight or tool].
Still happy to jump on a quick call if helpful."

DAY 21:
Channel: Call or Voicemail
"[Name], [Your Name] from [Company]. I've been thinking about what you shared 
regarding [their problem]. I want to make sure I help you figure out 
if there's a fit here — it would only take 10 minutes. 
I've got [Time 1] and [Time 2] this week. Which works better?"

DAY 30+:
Channel: Email (monthly)
Long-term nurture: industry insight, new case study, relevant update
Keep door open without pressure

NO-SHOW RE-ENGAGEMENT:
"[Name] — looks like we missed each other at [time]. No worries. 
I have [Time 1] and [Time 2] open this week — which works better?"
[Max 3 attempts before removing from active sequence]
```

## ANTI-PATTERNS & COMPLIANCE GUARDRAILS

### Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Hormozi's Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Accepting "I'll think about it" | Prospect ghosts; momentum dies | Ask "What would you need to think about?" |
| Talking after prospect says YES | Creates doubt, reopens objections | Shut up and move to payment immediately |
| Pitching before completing discovery | Talking at them, not to them | Always complete C+L+O before S |
| Manufactured scarcity ("Only 2 left!") | Destroys trust permanently if untrue | Only use real constraints |
| Answering detail questions blindly | May box you in or create confusion | "How many were you looking for?" |
| Over-explaining "how it works" | People want vacation, not travel plan | Stay at destination level |
| Pushing after hard no | Burns relationship, reputational risk | Accept gracefully, set future date |
| Skipping post-sale reinforcement | Buyer's remorse → churn/chargebacks | Celebrate their decision immediately |
| Starting call with no agenda | Rambling → loss of authority and control | Always open with Proof-Promise-Plan or ANOT |
| Fake authority close | "Trust me, it'll work" without basis | Use real client stories + data |

### Compliance & Ethics Guardrails

**Urgency / Scarcity:**
- All scarcity claims must be factually accurate (FTC §5 — deceptive acts/practices)
- Document real capacity limits or bonus expiration dates internally
- Countdown timers that reset = deceptive trade practice in most jurisdictions
- Price increases must be real — if extended, publicly acknowledge why

**Objection Handling:**
- AAA "Associate" must use real stories — do not fabricate testimonials
- Do not use "authority" framing to misrepresent relationships ("Alex himself told me...")
- Never create false urgency based on fabricated competing buyers

**Discovery:**
- Do not "lead" prospects to confirm problems they don't actually have
- Discovery questions are diagnostic, not manipulative — goal is fit assessment, not manufacturing need

**Follow-Up:**
- Respect "remove me from your list" immediately (CAN-SPAM, GDPR, TCPA)
- No-show re-engagement: 3 attempts max before removing from active sequence
- SMS/text follow-up requires prior written consent in US (TCPA)

**General:**
- Hormozi's philosophy: sell only what you genuinely believe will help the prospect
- "If you wouldn't be happy for a family member to buy your product, don't sell it" (paraphrased from his content)
- Qualification is ethical gatekeeping — it protects prospects from buying a bad fit as much as it protects your time
