# 14 CFR Part 145 - Repair Stations

**Source:** CFR-2025-title14-vol3.xml
**Effective:** January 1, 2025
**Authority:** 49 U.S.C. 106(g), 40113, 44701-44702, 44707, 44709, 44717

---

## Overview

Part 145 describes how to obtain a repair station certificate and contains the rules a certificated repair station must follow related to its performance of maintenance, preventive maintenance, or alterations of an aircraft, airframe, aircraft engine, propeller, appliance, or component part to which Part 43 applies.

---

## Subpart A - General

### § 145.1 Applicability
Describes how to obtain a repair station certificate and the rules certificated repair stations must follow for maintenance, preventive maintenance, or alterations of aircraft, airframes, engines, propellers, appliances, or component parts under Part 43.

### § 145.3 Definition of terms
Key definitions:
- **Accountable manager**: Person designated by repair station responsible for all Part 145 operations, ensuring personnel follow regulations, primary FAA contact
- **Article**: Aircraft, airframe, aircraft engine, propeller, appliance, or component part
- **Directly in charge**: Responsibility for work affecting airworthiness; must be available for consultation
- **Line maintenance**: Unscheduled maintenance from unforeseen events, or scheduled checks not requiring specialized training/equipment/facilities

### § 145.5 Certificate and operations specifications requirements
- No person may operate without valid certificate, ratings, or operations specifications
- Certificate and ops specs must be available on premises for public and FAA inspection

### § 145.12 Repair station records: Falsification, reproduction, alteration, or omission
Prohibits fraudulent entries, reproductions, alterations, or omissions in applications, records, or reports. Violations basis for certificate suspension/revocation, civil penalty, or application denial.

## Subpart B - Certification

### § 145.51 Application for certificate
Application requirements:
1. Repair station manual (§ 145.207)
2. Quality control manual (§ 145.211(c))
3. List of articles by type, make, or model
4. Organizational chart with personnel names/titles
5. Housing and facilities description (§ 145.103)
6. Contract maintenance functions list (§ 145.217)
7. **Training program for FAA approval (§ 145.163)**

### § 145.53 Issue of certificate
Certificate issued to persons meeting Subparts A-E requirements. Special provisions for bilateral aviation safety agreements and hazmat certification requirements.

### § 145.55 Duration and renewal of certificate
- US-located: Effective until surrendered, suspended, or revoked
- Outside US: Effective for 12 months, renewable for 24 months

### § 145.57 Amendment to or transfer of certificate
Required for name/location changes or rating additions/amendments.

### § 145.59 Ratings
Rating classes:
- **Airframe**: Class 1-4 (small/large, composite/metal)
- **Powerplant**: Class 1-3 (reciprocating ≤400hp, >400hp, turbine)
- **Propeller**: Class 1-2 (fixed-pitch, other)
- **Radio**: Class 1-3 (communication, navigation, radar)
- **Instrument**: Class 1-4 (mechanical, electrical, gyroscopic, electronic)
- **Accessory**: Class 1-3 (mechanical, electrical, electronic)

### § 145.61 Limited ratings
Ratings limited to specific make/model or type of article.

## Subpart C - Housing, Facilities, Equipment, Materials, and Data

### § 145.101 General
Housing and facilities must be acceptable to FAA and appropriate for work performed.

### § 145.103 Housing and facilities requirements
Must provide:
- Space for receiving, safekeeping, and protection of articles
- Segregated area for hazardous/flammable materials storage
- Adequate ventilation and lighting
- Environmental control for temperature-sensitive work

### § 145.105 Change of location, housing, or facilities
- Prior written FAA approval required for changes significantly affecting capability
- 30-day advance notification for non-significant changes

### § 145.107 Satellite repair stations
Provisions for satellite facilities operating under main certificate.

### § 145.109 Equipment, materials, and data requirements
Must have equipment, tools, and materials necessary for work performed. Technical data must be current and accessible.

---

## Subpart D - Personnel

### § 145.151 Personnel requirements
Adequate and trained personnel required for work performed.

### § 145.153 Supervisory personnel requirements
Must designate personnel to supervise maintenance functions and ensure Part 43 compliance.

### § 145.155 Inspection personnel requirements
Inspection personnel must:
- Understand inspection methods and procedures
- Be proficient in using inspection equipment
- Understand maintenance functions and articles inspected

### § 145.157 Personnel authorized to approve an article for return to service
Only appropriately certificated personnel may approve return to service.

### § 145.159 Recommendation of a person for certification as a repairman
Procedures for recommending employees for repairman certificates.

### § 145.160 Employment of former FAA employees
Restrictions on employing former FAA personnel.

### § 145.161 Records of management, supervisory, and inspection personnel
Must maintain rosters with:
- Name
- Address
- Airman certificate number (if applicable)
- Scope of employment
- Certificate type and ratings

Rosters must be updated within 5 business days of changes.

### § 145.163 Training requirements ⚠️ CRITICAL
**(a)** Must have FAA-approved employee training program with initial and recurrent training

**(b)** Training must ensure each employee assigned to maintenance, preventive maintenance, alterations, and inspection functions is capable of performing assigned tasks

**(c)** Must document individual employee training in FAA-acceptable format. **Records retained minimum 2 years.**

**(d)** Must submit training program revisions to responsible Flight Standards office per § 145.209(e)

**Regulatory Language:** "must have", "must ensure", "must document" = MANDATORY

### § 145.165 Hazardous materials training
- Hazmat employers must meet 49 CFR Part 172 Subpart H training requirements
- Employees performing Part 121/135 functions must be trained per operator's FAA-approved hazmat program

## Subpart E - Operating Rules

### § 145.201 Privileges and limitations of certificate
Privileges:
- Perform maintenance, preventive maintenance, alterations per Part 43 within ratings
- Arrange for others to perform work (must ensure QC system compliance if non-certificated)
- Approve return to service for rated articles

Limitations:
- May not work on unrated articles
- May not work on articles requiring unavailable data/equipment/facilities
- Return to service only after work per approved technical data

### § 145.203 Work performed at another location
May transport material/equipment/personnel for off-site work if:
- Work necessary due to special circumstance (FAA determined), OR
- Recurring basis with manual procedures for off-site work

### § 145.205 Maintenance for Part 121, 125, 135, and Part 129 operators
Must follow operator's maintenance program and applicable manual sections.

### § 145.206 Notification of hazardous materials authorizations
Must acknowledge operator notification and notify employees of operator's hazmat authorizations.

### § 145.207 Repair station manual
- Must prepare and follow FAA-acceptable manual
- Must maintain current manual
- Manual must be accessible to required personnel
- Must provide current manual to responsible Flight Standards office
- Must notify Flight Standards office of revisions

### § 145.209 Repair station manual contents
Required contents:
- (a) Organizational chart with management positions, responsibilities, authority
- (b) Procedures for maintaining/revising personnel rosters (§ 145.161)
- (c) Description of housing, facilities, equipment, materials
- (d) Procedures for capability list revisions (§ 145.215)
- (e) **Procedures for training program revisions (§ 145.163)**
- (f) Procedures for work at another location (§ 145.203)
- (g) Procedures for Part 121/125/135/129 maintenance (§ 145.205)
- (h) Procedures for contract maintenance (§ 145.217)
- (i) Description of recordkeeping system
- (j) Procedures for manual revisions

### § 145.211 Quality control system
Must have quality control system that:
- Inspects incoming raw materials/parts
- Performs preliminary, hidden damage, and final inspections
- Ensures calibrated tools and equipment
- Establishes recall system for defective articles
- Documents inspection findings

### § 145.213 Inspection of maintenance, preventive maintenance, or alterations
Procedures for inspecting work performed.

### § 145.215 Capability list
Must maintain capability list of articles for which maintenance can be performed.

### § 145.217 Contract maintenance
Requirements for work performed by others under contract.

### § 145.219 Recordkeeping
Must maintain records of work performed, including:
- Identity of article
- Description of work performed
- Date completed
- Approval for return to service signature

### § 145.221 Service difficulty reports
Must report certain failures, malfunctions, and defects.

### § 145.223 FAA inspections
Must allow FAA inspection at any time.
