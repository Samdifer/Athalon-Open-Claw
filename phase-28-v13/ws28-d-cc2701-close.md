---

### 3.2 Engine 2 — PCE-54210

**Inspection method:** Same as Engine 1.
**Components inspected:** Same as Engine 1.

**Findings:**

| Area | Finding | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Disc bore — forward face | No indications | No action required |
| Disc bore — aft face | No indications | No action required |
| Blade attachment slots (all 26 slots) | Slot 14: linear indication, 0.8mm length | **Evaluate per PWC limits** |
| Disc rim — leading edge quadrant | No indications | No action required |
| Disc rim — trailing edge quadrant | No indications | No action required |

**Slot 14 finding — detail:**

The linear indication at blade attachment slot 14 measured 0.8mm in length, oriented parallel to the slot centerline. P&WC SB PWC3-72-21051 acceptance criteria specify: blade slot indications up to 1.5mm are acceptable after re-inspection at 200 hours. Indications over 1.5mm require immediate removal.

**Inspector notation — Engine 2:** "Linear FPI indication found at blade attachment slot 14, 0.8mm length. Within PWC SB PWC3-72-21051 continued-service limits (1.5mm threshold). Disposition per SB: acceptable for continued service; re-inspect at 200 hours TIS from this inspection date. Re-inspection flag applied."

**Engine 2 disposition:** RETURN TO SERVICE WITH MONITORING — indication found within serviceable limits. Re-inspect at 200 hours TIS (next inspection due at aircraft hours: TBD per WO-DST-FPI-001 sign-off hours + 200).

---

### 3.3 Frank's Finding Assessment

> "Engine 1 was completely clean. Engine 2 had one indication — 0.8mm, within limits, with a 200-hour re-inspection flag. This is a common finding pattern for engines that have accumulated meaningful hours. The fact that the slot 14 indication was within limits and caught by FPI is exactly why this inspection exists. It's a precursor finding — the kind of thing that becomes a problem if you fly another 800 hours without looking.
>
> The prior DOM's failure to track this repetitive interval is what created the 447-hour overrun. If we had done this inspection on time (at 400 hours post-overhaul), we would have caught the slot 14 indication at a slightly smaller size. It's still within limits now. It's a lesson about why repetitive AD tracking matters."
