# Round 1 Output — Competitor And Adjacent Benchmark Analyst

## Scope

This output grounds round-1 design research in real products. It focuses on
workflow presentation and product posture more than on pure feature inventory.

## MRO Product Patterns

### CAMP

[CAMP eWorkOrder](https://www.campsystems.com/eWorkOrder) reinforces a pattern
Athelon should preserve: due planning, work-package creation, desktop review,
and mobile execution belong to one operational chain. The design implication is
that scheduling, work orders, and field execution should feel tightly connected.

### Quantum MX

[Quantum MX](https://www.quantum-mx.com/) represents a wide-coverage,
browser-first utility pattern. It emphasizes low-friction access, broad feature
coverage, and a practical “get work done in a browser” posture rather than a
heavy premium visual system. `Inference`: this matters because Athelon competes
not only on rigor, but on whether users can get value quickly.

### CORRIDOR

[CORRIDOR](https://corridor.aero/) and the current CAMP-hosted product
positioning show a deep maintenance-workflow, customer-satisfaction, and
business-operations framing. The design implication is that maintenance software
is judged partly on workflow completeness and operator trust, not just UI polish.

## Adjacent Product Patterns

### Odoo Shop Floor

[Odoo Shop Floor](https://www.odoo.com/documentation/18.0/applications/inventory_and_mrp/manufacturing/shop_floor.html)
is the strongest adjacent reference in this round. It highlights operator- and
station-centered execution rather than office-style navigation. This supports a
more purpose-built technician surface inside Athelon.

### SAP/Fiori-style enterprise flow

Even outside aviation, [SAP Fiori](https://experience.sap.com/fiori-design-web/)
shows that high-density transactional work benefits from role-based floorplans,
clear overview/detail separation, and fewer aesthetic flourishes. This is a
strong adjacent benchmark for billing, settings, reporting, and management work.

## What These Products Are Optimizing For

| Product family | Primary optimization |
|---|---|
| CAMP | Operational continuity across due planning, work packaging, and execution |
| Quantum MX | Low-friction, broad-coverage browser utility |
| CORRIDOR | Workflow depth and business relevance for maintenance organizations |
| Odoo Shop Floor | Station/operator execution efficiency |
| SAP/Fiori-style enterprise tools | Predictable, role-based transactional work |

## Translation Notes For Athelon

### 1. Do not separate planning and execution too aggressively

The MRO benchmark consistently suggests that users want planning, work-package
creation, and execution to feel related, not like disconnected subsystems.

### 2. Rigor is necessary but not sufficient

Athelon’s regulatory and audit depth is a differentiator, but the Quantum MX
benchmark suggests users still evaluate utility and immediacy.

### 3. Technician surfaces should feel more purpose-built than office surfaces

Odoo’s shop-floor posture supports a stronger distinction between execution
surfaces and administrative surfaces.

### 4. Commercial and customer communication surfaces should not inherit the full severity of compliance surfaces

The benchmark makes a strong case for keeping sales/CRM and customer-facing
communication lighter than work-order, compliance, and release flows.

## Handoff To Opus Synthesis

The benchmark lane supports a redesign family rather than a monolith:

1. operationally tight and serious where aircraft work is happening
2. lighter and clearer where business coordination is happening
3. browser-practical across the whole product
