# Private Equity Data Room Diligence: What Firms Actually Need When Evaluating Fortune 500 Acquisition Targets

*A practitioner-oriented reference covering all major workstreams, from the perspective of PE associates, VPs, and operating partners doing the actual work.*

---

## Framing: What PE Diligence Is Actually Trying to Do

The diligence process has three simultaneous objectives:

1. **Underwrite the deal** — validate (or break) the investment thesis, stress-test the financial model, and establish a defensible entry price
2. **Identify risk** — find anything that could destroy value post-close: hidden liabilities, key-person dependencies, regulatory exposure, structural problems
3. **Build the 100-day plan** — identify the operational levers that will generate returns

These objectives run in parallel. A well-structured data room serves all three.

## Part 1: Due Diligence Workstreams

### 1.1 Financial Diligence

**Who runs it:** The deal team (associates and VP) plus a Big 4 or accounting advisory firm conducting a formal Quality of Earnings (QoE) engagement.

**What they need from the data room:**

- Audited financial statements for at least 3–5 years (GAAP, with all footnotes)
- Monthly and quarterly management accounts for the trailing 36 months minimum
- Trial balance and general ledger access (ideally exportable, not just PDF)
- Revenue disaggregated by: customer, product/segment, geography, channel, contract type
- Cost structure detail: COGS by component, SG&A by department, R&D spend
- Customer-level revenue data going back at minimum 3 years
- Backlog and deferred revenue schedules
- Accounts receivable aging schedule and bad debt history
- Capital expenditure schedule with maintenance vs. growth capex broken out
- Working capital history by component (AR, inventory, AP, accruals) — monthly, not just year-end
- Cash conversion cycle data
- Intercompany eliminations and related-party transactions
- Compensation structure for senior management
- Board-approved budgets and forecasts for the next 2–3 years
- Most recent bank credit agreement and covenant compliance certificates
- Lease schedules (post-ASC 842 presentation)
- Any off-balance-sheet items or commitments
- Tax returns for 5 years

**What makes an associate's job easier vs. harder:**

*Easier:* Consistent chart of accounts across periods; clean month-end closes; revenue data provided in Excel not just PDFs; footnotes that actually explain non-recurring items; prior M&A integration costs cleanly labeled.

*Harder:* Restated financials without explanation; revenue recognition policies that changed mid-period without disclosure; intercompany transactions that haven't been eliminated; Q4 "sweeps" that dramatically adjust quarterly patterns; auditor change within the diligence period.

### 1.2 Quality of Earnings (QoE)

QoE is an independent reconstruction of normalized, sustainable EBITDA.

**Revenue Bridge Requirements:**

The accounting advisor needs to build a revenue bridge explaining changes from Year 1 → Year 2 → Year 3, broken into:
- Organic volume growth (same-customer, same-product)
- Price/mix impact
- New customer wins
- Customer churn (explicit, not buried in "other")
- New product introductions
- Acquisitions (revenue from acquired companies must be broken out — PE will haircut this)
- Divestitures and discontinued operations
- Foreign currency translation effects

**EBITDA Normalization — Common Adjustments PE Firms Will Dispute:**

- Management fees paid to parent
- Executive compensation above/below market
- "One-time" items that repeat every year
- Stock-based compensation (PE typically includes as an expense)
- Revenue from contracts that do not survive change of control
- Synergy adjustments — any "run-rate" cost saves not yet achieved are given zero credit
- EBITDA contributions from businesses being carved out
- Accounting policy differences from peers (LIFO vs. FIFO, capitalization thresholds)

**Working Capital Peg:**

This is a major economic issue. The data room needs:
- Monthly working capital by component for the trailing 24 months at minimum
- Seasonal working capital patterns with explanation
- AR factoring or securitization programs
- Supply chain finance programs that artificially inflate AP
- Customer prepayments or deposits that affect timing
- Inventory write-down history and reserves

**Customer Concentration Analysis:**

- Revenue by customer (top 25 customers by name, rest grouped)
- Customer tenure and tenure cohort analysis
- Contract expiration dates for top 20 customers
- Volume commitments (contractual minimums vs. aspirational)
- Win/loss data for recent renewals
- Price escalation clauses or lack thereof
- Customer credit quality and payment history
- Customers subject to change-of-control notification or consent requirements

### 1.3 Legal Diligence

**Who runs it:** Outside counsel retained by the PE firm (Kirkland & Ellis, Latham, Simpson Thacher).

**Corporate Structure:**
- Certificate of incorporation/articles, bylaws, organizational documents for every entity
- Capitalization table and equity ownership records
- Subsidiary structure chart with jurisdiction of formation for each entity
- Foreign qualification filings

**Material Contracts:**
- Every contract above a specified threshold (typically $1M+ annual value or 1–2% of revenue)
- Top 20 customer agreements, top 20 supplier/vendor agreements, all partnership and JV agreements
- **Change of control provisions** are the highest priority — counsel will review every material contract for consent rights, termination rights, or price renegotiation triggers
- Most favored nation provisions
- Exclusivity arrangements
- Non-compete and non-solicitation agreements
- IP licensing agreements (in and out)

**Litigation and Claims:**
- All pending or threatened litigation, arbitration, and regulatory proceedings
- Historical settlement agreements and consent decrees
- EEOC charges, wage/hour class actions, product liability matters
- Environmental enforcement actions
- Government investigations (DOJ, FTC, SEC, EPA, OSHA, state AGs)
- Patent infringement claims and inter partes review proceedings

**Regulatory and Compliance:**
- Licenses and permits required to operate (and which have change-of-control restrictions)
- Government contracts (ITAR, DFARS, FAR compliance)
- Anti-corruption/FCPA compliance program documentation
- OFAC sanctions compliance
- Export control compliance
- Privacy policy and GDPR/CCPA compliance documentation

---

### 1.4 Tax Diligence

- Federal, state, and foreign income tax returns for 5 years
- Tax provision workpapers (ASC 740 analysis)
- Transfer pricing documentation and intercompany agreements
- R&D tax credit calculations
- Section 382 NOL limitation analysis
- Sales and use tax nexus analysis and compliance history
- VAT/GST registration and compliance history for foreign subsidiaries
- Open tax audits, IDRs, or audit adjustments
- Private letter rulings, closing agreements
- Deferred tax asset schedules with realizability assessment
- Section 163(j) interest deduction limitation analysis
- GILTI/FDII/BEAT exposure for international operations
- Entity classification elections

**Key PE-specific tax issues at Fortune 500 scale:**
- Installment sale eligibility
- Section 338(h)(10) election availability (asset step-up)
- NOL carryforwards and limitations
- Tax receivable agreements from prior transactions
- Uncertain tax positions (UTPs) under ASC 740-10

---

### 1.5 Commercial Diligence

**Who runs it:** The deal team plus a strategy consultant (McKinsey, Bain, L.E.K.).

- Market size and growth data
- Competitive landscape documentation
- Pricing analysis — actual realized prices vs. list prices; discount approval authority
- Win/loss analysis with reason codes
- Customer satisfaction data: NPS scores, churn data, renewal rates, expansion revenue rates
- Sales force productivity metrics: quota attainment by rep, average deal size, sales cycle length
- Customer acquisition cost by channel
- Cohort revenue analysis (LTV trajectory)
- Product roadmap documentation
- Pricing power test: when was the last price increase, what was the magnitude, what was the customer reaction?

**For Fortune 500 carve-outs:**
- Revenue attributable to shared services or captive customers within the parent
- Transfer pricing arrangements that affect reported segment profitability
- Contracts that are enterprise-wide agreements naming the parent (not the target entity)

---

### 1.6 Operational Diligence

**Who runs it:** Operating partners plus specialized operations consultants.

- Facility footprint (owned vs. leased, square footage, utilization rates)
- Manufacturing/operations processes documentation
- Supply chain structure: tier-1 and tier-2 supplier concentration, sole-source dependencies
- Operational KPIs: throughput, yield, OEE, defect rates, order fulfillment rates
- Quality metrics: customer returns, warranty claims, field failures
- Capex history and capital intensity benchmarks vs. peers
- Maintenance schedules and deferred maintenance backlog
- Lean/Six Sigma or other process improvement history
- Procurement spend by category with vendor concentration
- Logistics costs as percent of revenue
- SKU rationalization history and current SKU count
- Overhead allocation methodology (relevant for carve-outs)

---

### 1.7 Information Technology Diligence

**Infrastructure and Architecture:**
- Current IT infrastructure diagram (on-premise, cloud, hybrid)
- Cloud provider relationships with annual spend
- Network architecture documentation
- Hardware inventory and refresh schedule
- Data center footprint
- Disaster recovery and business continuity plans (with most recent test results)

**Applications and Tech Stack:**
- Enterprise applications inventory: ERP, CRM, HRIS, supply chain management
- Custom-built applications and their criticality
- Integration architecture between systems
- System age and end-of-life status
- Software license agreements and compliance status
- Pending system implementations or major upgrades

**Tech Debt Assessment:**
- Age of core ERP (end-of-life ERP is typically $50–200M+ to replace for a Fortune 500)
- Percentage of infrastructure running on end-of-life operating systems
- Number of manual workarounds
- Development velocity metrics
- Developer headcount and attrition

**Cybersecurity Posture:**
- Most recent penetration test report and remediation status
- SOC 2 Type II reports
- ISO 27001 certification status
- Cybersecurity incident history (last 3 years)
- Cyber insurance policy coverage limits and exclusions
- EDR deployment, MFA rollout status, PAM controls
- Third-party vendor security assessment program
- Board-level cybersecurity reporting cadence

**Data Privacy Compliance:**
- GDPR compliance program documentation
- CCPA/CPRA compliance
- Data mapping inventory
- Data retention and deletion policies
- History of data breaches and regulatory action
- Privacy impact assessment process

---

### 1.8 Human Resources Diligence

**Organizational Structure:**
- Full org chart by department and reporting line (not just top 3 levels)
- Headcount by function, location, and employment type
- Spans and layers analysis relative to industry benchmarks
- Headcount history for 3 years: hires, terminations, voluntary attrition by level and function

**Compensation and Benefits:**
- Total compensation report by employee or at minimum by band/level
- Base salary benchmarking vs. market
- Annual and long-term incentive plan design and payout history
- Equity compensation outstanding with vesting schedules and triggering conditions
- Pension obligations (PBGC obligations, underfunded status, actuarial reports)
- Post-retirement medical benefits
- Benefits cost per employee vs. peer benchmarks
- Workers' compensation claims history and premiums

**Key Person Risk:**
- Identification of the 15–25 people who would most impair the business if they left
- Employment agreements for all C-suite and direct reports
- Non-compete enforceability analysis by state
- Retention agreement status for key persons
- "Leaver" provisions in equity agreements
- CEO/CFO assessment

**Labor Relations:**
- Union contracts: terms, expiration dates, historical grievance levels, strike history
- NLRB charges, unfair labor practice charges
- Workforce location by union/non-union status

**Culture Indicators:**
- Glassdoor ratings
- Employee engagement survey results (last 2 years)
- Voluntary attrition by level
- Exit interview data

---

### 1.9 Environmental Diligence

- Property inventory: all owned and leased real estate
- Phase I Environmental Site Assessments for all owned properties
- Phase II reports if Phase I identified recognized environmental conditions
- RCRA and CERCLA compliance history
- Hazardous materials usage and disposal history
- EPA enforcement actions, consent decrees, NOVs
- Air emissions permits, water discharge permits
- Underground storage tank history
- Brownfield or remediation obligations
- Asbestos and lead paint survey reports
- Environmental insurance policies
- Regulatory agency correspondence

---

### 1.10 Insurance Diligence

- Complete schedule of all insurance policies
- Policy limits, deductibles, exclusions for each line
- Claims history (5 years minimum) by line
- Self-insurance programs and captive insurance arrangements
- Insurance cost as percent of revenue vs. peer benchmarks
- Pending claims that may exhaust policy limits
- R&W insurance capacity assessment

## Part 2: Value Creation Lens — Building the 100-Day Plan During Diligence

### Revenue Growth Levers

- **Pricing power:** Has the company systematically underpriced vs. competitors?
- **Go-to-market gaps:** Sales force productivity below benchmarks; underpenetrated verticals
- **Cross-sell/upsell:** Customer wallet share analysis
- **International expansion:** Revenue mix vs. international addressable market
- **New product commercialization:** Products in development being under-resourced

### Cost Structure Levers

- **Procurement consolidation:** Supplier spend concentration and evidence of strategic sourcing
- **Overhead ratios:** G&A as a percent of revenue vs. pure-play peers
- **Outsourcing opportunities:** Functions that PE-backed peers have outsourced
- **Real estate footprint:** Office and facility rationalization opportunities
- **Headcount vs. revenue growth:** Headcount growth that outpaced revenue growth suggests a bloated organization

### Capital Efficiency

- **Working capital improvement:** DSO/DPO vs. industry peers. A 5-day improvement in DSO at a $2B revenue company frees $27M in cash.
- **Capex intensity:** Under-investing (deferred capex obligation) or over-investing vs. peers?
- **Portfolio pruning:** Non-core assets or business lines that could be sold at favorable multiples

### Management Quality Signals PE Teams Look For

- Does the management team understand unit economics? Can the CFO walk through a customer cohort analysis?
- Is the management team's forecast consistently directionally accurate?
- How does the CEO respond to bad news?
- Does the company have a performance management culture?
- What is the average tenure of direct reports to the CEO?
- Is there a layer of talent below the C-suite capable of stepping up?

## Part 3: Legal and Regulatory — Fortune 500-Scale Issues

### Antitrust Considerations

- Hart-Scott-Rodino (HSR) Act filing is nearly always required at Fortune 500 deal sizes
- Market share data by relevant antitrust market
- Products and services that overlap with existing PE portfolio companies
- The FTC and DOJ have been more aggressive on PE "buy-and-build" consolidation strategies
- Parallel merger control filings may be required (EU, UK CMA, China SAMR)

### Change of Control — The Single Biggest Legal Workstream

For a Fortune 500 company, change of control issues can be economically material:

- **Customer contracts:** Major contracts with consent rights
- **Government contracts:** DFARS and FAR novation requirements (90–180 days)
- **Debt agreements:** Change-of-control provisions triggering required buyback offers
- **Real estate leases:** Landlord consent requirements
- **Software licenses:** Enterprise licenses (SAP, Oracle, Salesforce) almost always have anti-assignment clauses — often a $5–30M+ issue
- **Joint ventures:** Partner right of first refusal or approval rights
- **Supplier agreements:** Sole-source suppliers with termination rights

## Part 4: ESG Considerations

### Environmental
- GHG emissions data (Scope 1, 2, and 3) — PE firms model carbon costs at $50–100/ton
- Net zero commitments the PE firm will inherit
- Physical climate risk for key facilities
- PFAS, BPA, and other materials under regulatory scrutiny

### Social/HR
- DEI data: workforce composition by race/ethnicity and gender at each level
- Pay equity analysis
- Supply chain labor practices and tier-1 supplier audits
- Safety record: OSHA recordable incident rates vs. industry benchmarks

### Governance
- Board composition: independence, diversity, financial expertise
- Related-party transactions
- Executive compensation governance
- Whistleblower policy and hotline utilization
- Code of conduct and ethics training completion rates

## Part 5: Timeline Expectations

### Phase 0: Teaser / Executive Summary (Weeks 1–2)
2–5 pages. Company name typically blind. Revenue range, EBITDA, industry, and geography.

### Phase 1: CIM + Phase 1 Data Room (Weeks 3–8)
After NDA execution. CIM is 80–150 pages. Phase 1 data room contains audited financials, management presentations, summary customer list, material contracts, org chart, litigation summary.

**Management Presentations:** 4–8 hours in person. Critical event — simultaneously diligence and relationship-building.

### Phase 2: Full Data Room Access (Weeks 8–16)
After indicative offers. All workstream advisors go to work simultaneously. Full contract database, complete financial detail, customer-level data, employee data, environmental assessments, IT documentation.

### Phase 3: Confirmatory Diligence (Weeks 16–22)
After final bids and exclusivity. Customer-specific data, key employee files, full environmental reports, tax return detail, regulatory correspondence. R&W insurance underwriting.

**Total Timeline:**

| Phase | Duration |
|---|---|
| Teaser to first-round bids | 6–10 weeks |
| First-round to final bids (full diligence) | 8–12 weeks |
| Exclusivity to signing | 4–8 weeks |
| Signing to closing (regulatory clearances) | 3–6 months |
| **Total from process launch to close** | **7–12 months** |

### What Compresses or Extends the Timeline

**Compressors:** Vendor Due Diligence (VDD) report prepared upfront; management responsive to Q&A in 24–48 hours; organized data room with standard index; early HSR threshold analysis.

**Extenders:** Disorganized data room; limited management Q&A sessions; financial restatements; unexpected regulatory issues; key management departures; financing market dislocation.

## Part 6: What Makes Each Role's Job Easier vs. Harder

### For Associates

*Easier:* Logical folder hierarchy; maintained index/tracker; consistent naming conventions; Excel files in native format; dedicated sell-side coordinator with 24-hour Q&A SLA; proactive flagging of issues early.

*Harder:* Missing documents without explanation; conflicting financial data; scanned images of contracts; data room organized by upload date; management answering "as we said in the CIM."

### For VPs

*Easier:* Pre-agreed workstream advisor scopes; properly staged data room; management willing to explain the business.

*Harder:* IT and HR data not available until confirmatory diligence; adversarial sell-side counsel; management projections assuming perpetual 15% CAGR.

### For Operating Partners

*Easier:* Granular operational data; access to plant managers and mid-level operations leaders; company benchmarking work; functional leaders with clear views on under-investment.

*Harder:* All data filtered through one protective CFO; KPI definitions that change year over year; no cost accounting by product or facility.

## Part 7: Red Flags That Kill or Reprice Deals

1. **Customer concentration with contract risk**: Top customer >25% of revenue AND has change-of-control consent right with expressed dissatisfaction
2. **Undisclosed regulatory investigation**
3. **Environmental liabilities exceeding $50M at unowned sites** (CERCLA successor liability)
4. **Material cybersecurity incident not disclosed**
5. **Pension liability significantly underfunded** ($200M+ funding gap)
6. **Revenue not owned post-separation**: Revenue from shared services, enterprise agreements, or captive customers
7. **Key person risk without mitigation**: CEO/CTO indicating departure with no succession plan
8. **IT infrastructure requiring immediate replacement**: ERP upgrade exceeding $100M within 12 months
9. **FCPA or corruption exposure**: Third-party payments without compliance program
10. **QoE comes back 20%+ below management's EBITDA**
