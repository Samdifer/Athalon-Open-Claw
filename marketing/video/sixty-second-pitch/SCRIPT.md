# AVLY.IO / Athelon — 60-Second Pitch Video Script

**Format:** 1920x1080 | 30fps | 1800 frames | 60 seconds
**Style:** Grassroots indie startup. Fast-paced. Authentic. Punchy.
**Tone:** Two real mechanics who've been in the trenches — not slick corporate.
**Music:** Driving indie/electronic beat — builds energy through the video.

---

## Polished Script (60-Second Cut)

### SCENE 1 — THE HOOK (0:00–0:06 | Frames 0–180)

**Visual:** Black screen. Bold white text slams in one line at a time. Each line hits like a punch. Minimal. High contrast.

> I'm Sam. This is Nate.
> Two aircraft mechanics.
> Eleven years on the flight line.

**Director's note:** No easing in. Text appears hard and fast — `spring()` with high stiffness. This sets the tone: we're not here to waste your time.

---

### SCENE 2 — CREDIBILITY (0:06–0:14 | Frames 180–420)

**Visual:** Quick-cut silhouettes of military aircraft, A&P license graphic, hangar environments. Text slides in from the right over dark imagery.

> Nate came up through the military — heavy iron, combat readiness.
> I got my A&P and cut my teeth on Part 135 charter ops.
> Between us? We've touched every corner of this industry.

**Director's note:** Establish trust fast. These aren't tech bros — they're wrench-turners who code. The imagery should feel real, not stock-photo.

---

### SCENE 3 — THE PROBLEM (0:14–0:22 | Frames 420–660)

**Visual:** Screen fills with a red-tinted cascade of pain points. Text rains down rapidly — almost too fast to read, creating a sense of overwhelm. Then one statement holds.

> And no matter where we went — same problems.
> Paperwork nightmares. Scheduling chaos.
> Compliance headaches that never end.
> Software built in the '90s... still running the show.

**Director's note:** This is the emotional core. The viewer should *feel* the frustration. The cascade creates visual overwhelm — matching the mechanic's daily reality. The last line ("'90s") should hold for a beat with a slight glitch effect.

---

### SCENE 4 — THE PIVOT (0:22–0:28 | Frames 660–840)

**Visual:** Hard tonal shift. Background transitions from dark/red to bright/clean. Forward-motion scale animation — like accelerating down a runway.

> Last year, we stopped complaining and started building.
> AI tools. Workflow fixes. Common-sense solutions.
> We made a difference inside the shops we worked in.
> Then we realized — this is way bigger than one shop.

**Director's note:** Energy turns from frustration to determination. The color shift should feel like a weight lifted. "Way bigger than one shop" is the pivot moment — give it visual emphasis.

---

### SCENE 5 — THE VISION (0:28–0:36 | Frames 840–1080)

**Visual:** Clean, expansive frame. AVLY.IO logo reveals with a satisfying spring animation. Tagline text fades in underneath. Breathing room.

> So we started AVLY.IO.
> One mission: bring sanity back to aircraft maintenance.
> Kill the daily headaches.
> Replace confusion with clarity.

**Director's note:** First moment of visual calm in the video. Let the logo breathe. The contrast with the previous chaos is intentional — this is what order looks like.

---

### SCENE 6 — THE PRODUCT (0:36–0:46 | Frames 1080–1380)

**Visual:** Athelon logo reveal (bigger, bolder than AVLY). Then UI mockup screenshots animate in — feature callout cards pop around them. Quick parade of capabilities.

> Meet Athelon — our first product.
> Planning. Quoting. Scheduling. Resources. Compliance.
> Part 145 and Part 135 — one platform.
> Powered by AI that actually understands your operation.

**Director's note:** This is the product money shot. Each feature word should pop with its own animation beat. The UI screenshots sell legitimacy — this is real software, not vaporware.

---

### SCENE 7 — THE VALUE (0:46–0:52 | Frames 1380–1560)

**Visual:** High-contrast black and white. Giant bold text. Each statement fills the screen. No subtlety.

> This isn't a software upgrade.
> It's a competitive advantage.
> Make more money. Run better maintenance. Period.

**Director's note:** Shortest scene. Maximum impact. The word "Period." should land with finality — full stop, no animation after.

---

### SCENE 8 — THE CTA (0:52–1:00 | Frames 1560–1800)

**Visual:** Urgency. The number "8" animates large, then shrinks into the sentence. URL pulses with a subtle glow. Lock icon suggests exclusivity.

> We're taking our first 8 customers.
> Test what we've built. Lock in founder pricing — forever.
> avly.io/athelon
> Grab your spot. The future of aviation maintenance starts now.

**Director's note:** Create FOMO. "8 customers" should feel scarce. The URL needs to be unmissable — it's the single action we want. End on energy, not a fade-out. Hard cut to black with the URL holding for 1 second.

---

## Word Count & Pacing

- Total words: ~230
- Target pace: ~3.8 words/second (natural, energetic speaking pace)
- Each scene's text is timed to its frame allocation

## Appendix: Original Full Dialog

The script above was refined from Sam's original raw dialog for timing and impact. Here is the unedited source material:

---

> I'm Sam, this is Nate. We are two aircraft mechanics who started working on airplanes eleven years ago. Nate was in the military and worked on these types of aircraft. Sam got his A&P license and worked on some of these types of airplanes. Both of us have done a bunch of different jobs in aviation and are frustrated with some of the problems that seem to pop up no matter where we are or what job we take.
>
> In the last year, we decided to try to give back to the industry and start building things for aircraft technicians and solving problems for the industry that just don't have answers right now.
>
> We are involved in a bunch of fun projects. First we built a lot of interesting tools for our prior jobs using AI as well as just some common sense and tried to make a difference within the organizations we were working with. Now we've decided to go full-time into building something we think is much bigger.
>
> We think there is an opportunity to finally bring some sanity back into aircraft operations and maintenance and to get rid of the daily headaches, confusion, and complications of a poorly organized maintenance environment and flight operation environment.
>
> So Nate and I started AVLY.IO and are currently building all of the things that we daydreamed about while we worked these different jobs.
>
> Specifically, we're taking the software that was written in the 1990s and bringing it all the way into the modern age with features that were just simply not possible a couple of years ago.
>
> So we introduce to you Athelon, our first product, which will organize all of your work: planning, quoting, scheduling, resources, and compliance for your Part 145 and Part 135 operations in one place.
>
> This will allow you to make in-the-moment decisions and handle enormous amounts of complexity by relying on common sense architecture and problem solving as well as the power of AI to understand and predict your biggest problems and solve them.
>
> This is an application that won't just replace your old software. It will let you make a lot more money and become much better at aircraft maintenance.
>
> Please check it out and get on our wait list at avly.io/athelon. We are taking our first customers. If you help us test what we've made and give us your opinions, you will be locked into a forever very low price. We're only taking eight initial customers so grab your spot now and join the cutting edge of aviation technology!
