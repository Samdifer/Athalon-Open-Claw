## Marcus Webb — Part 29 / S-76C Compliance Statement

*Filed as part of WS26-B artifact*

The Sikorsky S-76C (N76LS) at Lone Star Rotorcraft is certified under 14 CFR Part 29, Transport Category Rotorcraft. This creates the following compliance considerations relevant to WS26-B:

**Administrative work (work orders, task cards, parts, sign-offs):** GO. The Athelon work order structure is aircraft-agnostic. Work orders, task cards, parts receiving records, and IA sign-off functionality operate identically for Part 27 and Part 29 aircraft. No architectural changes are required to support the S-76C in Part 91 admin scope.

**Compliance surface (AD, ALS, LLP):** NOT YET ACTIVE for S-76C. The S-76C has its own ALS under 14 CFR §29.1529 and its own AD applicability profile (Sikorsky SILs, FAA ADs specific to the S-76 series). Neither has been audited for Athelon's data model. These are Phase 27 scope items.

**Part 29 distinction from Part 27:** Part 29 transport category rotorcraft have more extensive ICA requirements than Part 27 normal category. The ALS for the S-76C is likely more detailed and more heavily event-triggered than the R44 ALS. The alsItems data model designed in WS26-A accommodates Part 29 aircraft via the `ICA_ALS_PART29` regulatory basis enum value. The data model is ready; the audit is not.

**Verdict:** Part 91 administrative work for N76LS is GO. Compliance surface features are NOT activated pending S-76C audit. This is the correct posture.

*— Marcus Webb, Compliance Architect*
