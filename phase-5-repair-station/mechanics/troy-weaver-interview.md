# Interview — Troy Weaver
**Lead A&P Mechanic, Airframe Specialist**  
**Interviewers:** Nadia Solis (PM), Tanya Birch (Mobile/Offline)  
**Format:** 60-minute working session, SunState Jet Center break room, Lakeland FL  
**Date:** 2026-02-22  
**Transcribed and condensed by:** Nadia Solis

---

*Troy arrived early. He had a coffee in a cup that said "World's Okayest Mechanic" and he did not smile when he sat down, but he wasn't hostile either. He looked at Tanya's laptop and the voice recorder and said, "I've done one of these before. The company didn't build half of what we asked for." We noted that. He nodded and said "Okay."*

---

**Nadia:** Troy, let's start simple. Walk me through what a morning looks like for you when you've got a task card to work.

**Troy:** I'm usually here at six-thirty. Walk the hangar, see what's on jacks, confirm nothing changed overnight. Then I go to the system and pull up the work order. Right now that's Corridor. I've got a tab pinned for the shop's active WOs. I find my aircraft, I find my card for the day. If there's history on the card from a previous inspection — like I'm doing the same 300-hour task I did six months ago on the same airframe — I want to see what was found last time before I open anything. In Corridor, that's not one click. It's more like four, and you have to know to look for it. A lot of guys don't.

**Nadia:** Why does the history matter before you open the panel?

**Troy:** Because if Ray Kowalski wrote a finding note six months ago that said "slight fretting on the forward spar attachment" and I don't know about it, I'm doing an inspection without half my information. I might assess the fretting as new. I might assess it as not progressing. I can't assess it at all if I don't know it was there. The system should surface that to me. It shouldn't be something I have to go digging for.

**Nadia:** Should that be automatic? Every time you open a task card, the previous findings surface?

**Troy:** *(pause, three seconds, looks at ceiling)* Yes. Collapsed by default if there's nothing unusual. But if the last sign-off had a finding note, it should be visible without any action from me. The aircraft is telling me something. I want to hear it.

**Nadia:** What about the reference documents — the AMM sections? How do you use those today?

**Troy:** It depends on the task. For stuff I've done a hundred times, I know the procedure. I'm not pulling up the manual every time I torque a gear door hinge. But for anything non-routine, or anything on an airframe I'm less familiar with, I want the reference. Right now I've got a PDF library on the shop server and I use the iPad to pull up sections. It works. It's not seamless. I'm copying part numbers out of one window and cross-referencing them in another. It's fine. But —

*(He stops. Nadia waits.)*

**Troy:** I'll tell you what I actually want, since you're building the thing. I want the task card to link to the specific section. Not the document. The section. AMM 53-10-01, paragraph 4. When I tap the reference, it opens there. Not to page 1 of a 600-page PDF.

**Nadia:** That's a specific requirement. Do you have a sense of how often you're actually going to the manual mid-task?

**Troy:** On something like a Challenger annual? Ten, twelve times a day. Some of those are torque confirmations, five seconds each. Some of them are detailed procedural steps where I'm working through a sequence. If I have to navigate to the document myself every time, that adds up.

---

*Tanya Birch enters the conversation here.*

---

**Tanya:** Troy, I want to ask about the mobile side of this — the tablet and phone experience. When you're at the aircraft, what's your current setup?

**Troy:** Shop iPad. It lives on a cart we wheel around the hangar. It's not mine, it's the shop's. I use it to access Corridor, pull up manuals, check parts status. It's a good tablet, but the Corridor mobile experience is —

*(He shakes his head slightly.)*

**Troy:** Corridor's mobile is fine for reading. Not fine for doing. Signing a task card on the Corridor mobile is like trying to parallel park a semi. Technically possible. Not something you want to do regularly.

**Tanya:** What makes the sign-off bad on mobile specifically?

**Troy:** The confirm button is small. The authentication step — you have to type your password — is painful on a touchscreen. And after you do all of that, there's a little spinner and then the page reloads and you have to scroll back down to confirm it went through. I want it to be: I tap Sign, I see a clear summary of what I'm signing, I authenticate, and the card immediately shows "SIGNED — Troy Weaver — 14:32Z." Done. The confirmation should be immediate. Not a reload. Not a spinner that might mean it worked or might mean it didn't.

**Tanya:** What about connectivity? The hangar here — what's the WiFi like?

**Troy:** *(small laugh)* It's fine if you're near the front of the building. Back of the hangar, near the big door, it drops off. And if you're inside an aircraft on jacks — inside the cabin, in the avionics bay — you're basically offline. It's not bad WiFi, it's just metal walls.

**Tanya:** What should happen to an in-progress task if you lose connectivity mid-sign-off?

**Troy:** That is the question, right? That's the question nobody ever designs for. Here's what I need: I need to know. If I hit Sign and something happened with the connection, I need a clear message that tells me whether the signature went through or not. What I don't want is ambiguity. "Maybe it worked, go check later" is not acceptable. That is a maintenance record. That is my certificate. Tell me definitively what happened.

**Tanya:** Should the app let you sign while offline and queue it?

**Troy:** *(pause)* I've thought about this. Yes — with conditions. If I'm working through a 45-step task and I'm on step 38 and I lose connectivity, I should be able to finish steps 38 through 45 and have them queue. But when I reconnect, I want to see the queue — I want to see "5 signatures queued, syncing now" and then I want to see them confirm one by one. Not "sync complete" as a single message. I want to see each one clear. Because if step 39 has a problem and the rest went through, I need to know that specifically.

---

*Back to Nadia.*

---

**Nadia:** Let's talk about parts. When you're on a task card and the part you need for that card is either on order or hasn't been received — how does that affect your workflow?

**Troy:** Right now it's a separate lookup. I go to the parts screen, find the request, check the status. It's not tied to the card in any obvious way. So there's this constant back-and-forth. I shouldn't have to leave the card to know if the part is in the building.

**Nadia:** The requirement being: parts status is visible from the task card.

**Troy:** Yes. Not a separate click. Right there on the card. "Slick 4370 — On Order, ETA Feb 25." If it's been received and staged, I want to see that too. "Slick 4370 — In Storeroom, Bin 14A." That's the information I need at the aircraft.

**Nadia:** Anything else the card should surface?

**Troy:** One more thing. The sign-off summary — I want to see it before I authenticate. I don't want to click Sign and then have it ask me to authenticate and then confirm. Show me the summary first: this is the aircraft, this is the task, this is the reference, this is my cert number, this is the date. I read it. Then I authenticate. In that order. Because I've been burned by software that puts the authentication before the confirmation. You sign first and then see what you signed. That's backwards.

---

**Nadia:** Last question, Troy. Fill in the blank for me: "I wish the software would..."

**Troy:** *(without hesitation)* ...remember that I'm at the aircraft, not at a desk. Every single design decision — where the buttons are, how many taps something takes, what information is visible without scrolling — should start from the assumption that I'm standing in a hangar, I've got a flashlight in one hand, and I've got maybe 30 seconds before I need to put the phone down and get back to work. If the software doesn't fit in 30 seconds, it's not designed for me.

*(Pause.)*

**Troy:** One more. I wish it would show me the sign-off confirmation in a way that I can screenshot and send to myself if I ever need to prove it happened. Not because I don't trust the system. Because I don't know if I trust the system yet.

---

*Interview ended. Troy left his coffee cup. We took that as a good sign.*
