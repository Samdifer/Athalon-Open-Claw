## 5. Strengths — What Quantum MX Does Well

1. **Price**: $79-189/mo with unlimited users is industry-beating. Hard to undercut.
2. **Mature product**: Operating since 2013 — 13 years of refinement. Battle-tested.
3. **Document generation engine**: Their custom form-fill template system (Open Office → PDF) is genuinely powerful. Supports 337s, 8110-3s, 91.411 certs, W&B sheets, custom forms.
4. **Logbook stickers**: One-click logbook entry → print to Dymo labels. Shops love this.
5. **QuickBooks integration**: Deep, bidirectional — invoices, POs, sales receipts, inventory sync, payroll export.
6. **Digital signatures**: AC120-78B compliant ink-style signatures with proper locking and audit trail.
7. **Customer portal**: Clean workflow — customers can update flight times, view status, approve estimates, report squawks, view invoices.
8. **Parts traceability**: Full lifecycle from PO → receiving → inventory → installed on aircraft → core return.
9. **QR/barcode system**: Scan labels for inventory operations and clock-in. Proven in shop environments.
10. **Multi-team isolation**: Multiple P&L lines (A&P + avionics + instruments) from one domain.
11. **No training required**: Genuinely simple enough that small shops self-onboard.
12. **Real phone support**: Actual humans answering phones — rare in SaaS.
13. **Multi-currency**: Built-in with automatic exchange rates.
14. **Package tracking**: Auto-updates from FedEx/UPS/USPS.
15. **Registry imports**: Pull aircraft data from FAA and Canadian Transport.

### Structural Weaknesses

1. **No mobile app**: Browser-only. Responsive design isn't the same as native/PWA in a hangar with poor WiFi.
2. **No scheduling/Gantt**: Zero visual scheduling. No bay management. No capacity planning. This is a major gap for any shop with 3+ bays.
3. **No predictive analytics**: Can't forecast TAT, resource needs, or maintenance patterns beyond basic reminders.
4. **No real-time collaboration**: Messages are async. No live cursors, no real-time status updates.
5. **Support quality**: Mixed reviews. Some rave, others report rude emails and unhelpful responses. Small team = inconsistent.
6. **No API**: Can't integrate with anything outside their ecosystem (except QBO, Airbly, AirSync).
7. **Dated UI**: Website and screenshots suggest a functional but visually dated interface. No dark mode.
8. **Limited analytics**: Basic inventory analytics and estimate vs. actual. No P&L, no revenue trending, no financial projections.
9. **No compliance workflow**: No step-by-step task card flow. No precondition engine. No automated RTS authorization.
10. **No training management**: Can't track technician qualifications, certifications, or training requirements.
11. **No onboarding wizard**: Setup requires reading help docs and manual configuration.
12. **Export-limited**: Can download data listings but no sophisticated export/reporting engine.

## 10. Strategic Recommendations for Athelon

1. **Customer Portal** — QMX's strongest customer-facing feature. Athelon needs owner self-service (view status, approve estimates, update times).
2. **Document generation** — Custom form-fill templates for 337s, 8110-3s, condition tags. Without this, repair stations won't switch.

## 14. Key Takeaway

Quantum MX is a **solid, affordable, mature product** for **small GA shops** (1-10 employees). It excels at the paperwork side of maintenance — document generation, logbook entries, invoicing, and parts traceability.

**However**, it has **zero scheduling capability**, **no financial intelligence**, **no compliance workflow engine**, and a **dated UI**. For shops that need to plan work across multiple bays, manage technician assignments, or project P&L — Quantum MX falls short.

**Athelon's strategic position:** Beat QMX on capability (scheduling, compliance, financials, modern UX) while closing the gaps they do well (QBO, document generation, customer portal, logbook stickers). Price competitively against their $79-189 range and Athelon becomes the obvious upgrade path for growing shops.
