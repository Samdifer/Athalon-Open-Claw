## §4. Nadia's Recommendation — Consolidation vs. 8th Shop

**Recommendation: Authorize ONE inbound prospect for Phase 33 qualification, with a clear-eyed assessment of team readiness. Do not force a parallel dual-onboarding.**

**Rationale:**

*The team's actual bandwidth:*
Phase 32 delivered v1.4.0 — six features, seven shops, two open compliance items, a 30-day check-in, and a scoping session. That's full-team output for four weeks. Jonas's release pipeline, Marcus's compliance calendar, and Devraj's engineering queue are all at high utilization. Cilla just ran 148 test cases. We are not at slack capacity.

*Why not consolidate on current 7:*
The seven existing shops are stable. v1.4.0 is deployed. WS32-B closed both Ridgeline open items. WS32-C gave us a clear read on RMTS. The shops are not demanding active support that would preclude a new onboarding. The compliance infrastructure is now robust enough that an 8th shop does not represent the same risk it would have in Phase 21 (when the second shop onboarding nearly exposed gaps in the ALS template library). We have templates for C208B, TBM 850, Bell 206, S-76C, R44. We have a formal ALS audit protocol. The onboarding playbook is real.

*Why NOT both prospects at once:*
Lorena Vásquez (P-32-02) represents a substantially more complex onboarding than any prior shop. A King Air B200 with PT6A-42 engines, a dual-cert Part 145/135 operation, and a Part 135 ops spec that will stress-test our existing MEL handling — this is not a straightforward onboarding. Marcus will need 3–4 weeks to build the PT6A-42 template. The Part 135 integration work from Priya's shop is good, but a 7-aircraft King Air charter operation is a different compliance surface than a Beech 200 Part 91 owner.

Paul Kaminski (P-32-01) is a simpler fit — another C208B shop at KGJT, Dale Renfrow as a reference, PT6A-114A template already built. This one is low-friction and could run in parallel with Phase 33 planning. But "low-friction" still takes Nadia's time, Marcus's compliance walkthrough, and Jonas's deployment slot.

*Decision rationale:*
Prioritize Lorena Vásquez (P-32-02) as the Phase 33 primary onboarding candidate. The King Air B200 is strategically valuable — it unlocks a new aircraft type and a new compliance surface (Part 135 turboprop) that would position Athelon well for the larger charter market. Begin the PT6A-42 template build in Phase 33 planning. Run Paul Kaminski's outbound call as a parallel track, with the explicit understanding that his onboarding (if authorized) moves to Phase 34.

*On the question of v1.4.0 stabilization first:*
v1.4.0 is stable. The release was clean. The 7-shop rollout had zero incidents. One P2 cosmetic defect (sort order in the TBM 850 template view) is queued for a hotfix. That defect does not affect compliance or operations. We are not in a stabilization-required posture — we are in a growth-authorized posture.

**Bottom line:** Authorize P-32-02 (Lorena Vásquez, High Plains Aero) for Phase 33 prospect qualification. Hold P-32-01 (Paul Kaminski) as Phase 34 pipeline. Do not run simultaneous new-shop onboardings without a team capacity addition.
