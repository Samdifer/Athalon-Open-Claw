## The Sales Playbook (10 Customers, Not 100)

### This Is Not a Marketing Funnel. This Is 10 Conversations.

You don't need SEO. You don't need a pricing page. You don't need content marketing. You need **10 qualified conversations with decision-makers at shops that match the ICP.** That's it.

### Where to Find Them

**Source 1: FAA Repair Station Directory (Direct)**
- The FAA publishes every certificated Part 145 station: name, address, ratings, size indicators
- Filter for: GA/turbine ratings, US-based, indicators of 10+ mechanics (multiple ratings, large facility)
- Manually research the top 50 — LinkedIn, Google, trade directories — to identify owner/DOM name
- **Send 50 personalized emails. Not templates. Personalized.** Reference their specific ratings, aircraft types, location.
- Expected: 10–15 responses, 5–8 demos, 2–3 closes
- Timeline: Weeks 1–4

**Source 2: Trade Shows & IA Seminars (In Person)**
- MRO Americas, Sun 'n Fun, regional FSDO-sponsored IA renewal seminars
- You don't need a booth. Attend, network, buy people coffee.
- Target: meet 20 DOMs/owners in person over 6 months
- Expected: 5–10 demos, 2–4 closes
- Timeline: Ongoing

**Source 3: Warm Introductions**
- Every person in your network who touches aviation maintenance
- A&P schools, FBO operators, aircraft brokers, avionics shops — they all know Part 145 operators
- Ask specifically: "Do you know a repair station owner who's frustrated with their software or still on paper?"
- Expected: 10–15 intros, 5–8 demos, 2–3 closes
- Timeline: Weeks 1–8

**Source 4: Corridor Contract Renewal Timing**
- Corridor requires annual or multi-year commitments
- Shops evaluate alternatives 60–90 days before renewal
- The EBIS and Corridor user communities (forums, LinkedIn groups) surface complaints regularly
- Monitor and engage. Not with a sales pitch — with "I built something. Want to see it?"
- Expected: 3–5 conversations, 1–2 closes
- Timeline: Opportunistic

**You need 30–40 qualified conversations to close 10. That's 5–7 per month. One to two per week. Very doable for a founder.**

## The Sales Process (High-Touch, Not Self-Serve)

### Stage 1: Discovery (30 min)

Don't demo. Listen. Ask:
- What software are you on today? (or: how are you managing records?)
- When was your last FAA surveillance audit? How did it go?
- What's the one thing that wastes the most time in your shop every day?
- How many mechanics, how many active tails?
- What does your billing process look like — WO close to invoice, how long?

**Goal:** Confirm ICP fit. Identify the specific pain. If they're a 4-mechanic shop on EBIS paying $2K/yr and happy, disqualify and move on.

### Stage 2: Tailored Demo (45 min)

Show THEIR workflow, not your product:
- Create a work order for an aircraft type they actually work on
- Walk through task card → sign-off → parts issue → RTS — the exact sequence their mechanics do daily
- Show mobile access (their mechanic's phone, not a desktop)
- Show the audit trail (pull up a record and demonstrate the FAA inspection scenario)
- Show real-time updates (open two browser tabs — show data syncing instantly)

**The DOM will either lean forward or lean back in the first 10 minutes. If they lean forward, you have a deal. If they lean back, ask what's missing.**

### Stage 3: Compliance Review (The Closer)

This is the differentiator that justifies the $5K compliance review fee and closes the deal:
- Review their current compliance documentation (or lack thereof)
- Map their specific regulatory obligations (ratings, aircraft types, AD applicability)
- Show them exactly how Athelon captures each required record
- Walk through a simulated FAA audit: "Inspector asks for all maintenance records on N123AB for the past 24 months. Here's what that looks like in Athelon. Under 30 seconds."

**This is the moment the DOM decides. Not because the software is pretty — because it makes her defensible.**

### Stage 4: Proposal & Close

- Custom proposal: shop name, mechanic count, aircraft count, specific pricing
- Annual contract, paid upfront (offer 2-month discount for annual vs monthly)
- Implementation timeline: 2 weeks to full operation
- Include in the proposal: "Athelon guarantees your first FAA-compliant work order is completed within 5 business days of contract signing."
- **No negotiating on price. Negotiate on scope (add training hours, extend support, include compliance review). Never discount the platform fee.**

## Why 10 Customers Is Better Than 85

| Dimension | 85-Shop Plan | 10-Customer Plan |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue per customer | $8,200 | $38,000 |
| Sales motion | High-volume outbound + marketing | Founder-led consultative |
| Support load | 85 shops = full-time CSM by Month 3 | 10 shops = manageable solo |
| Churn risk | High — cheap customers churn easily | Low — high-touch, invested customers stay |
| Product feedback quality | Scattered, hard to prioritize | 10 deep relationships, clear signal |
| Referral quality | "Try this cheap tool" | "This is what my shop runs on" |
| Implementation complexity | Self-serve (must be bulletproof) | White-glove (you can fix issues in real time) |
| Brand positioning | Budget alternative | Professional-grade platform |
| Path to $1M ARR | Need 120+ shops | Need 15 shops |
| Fundraising story | "We have 85 SMB customers" | "Our 10 customers pay $72K/yr avg with 0% churn" |

The 10-customer plan builds a **premium brand** from Day 1. You can always move downmarket later. Moving upmarket after establishing yourself as the cheap option is nearly impossible.

## Risk Matrix

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Can't find 10 shops that fit the ICP | The FAA database has 4,300 Part 145 stations. 50 outreach attempts per batch. If <2% convert, the ICP is wrong — adjust. |
| Shops won't pay $72K/yr for unproven software | They're paying $42K–$80K for proven-but-hated software. Offer a 90-day exit clause (not a discount) to reduce risk. |
| Implementation takes longer than 2 weeks | You're doing it yourself for the first 4–6 customers. Control the timeline. Budget 3 weeks per shop and overlap them. |
| P0 engineering takes longer than 4 weeks | PDF generation is the only true blocker. Ship ugly PDFs first (functional, not beautiful). Polish in Month 2. |
| A customer churns in the first 6 months | At $38K/customer, one churn costs you $38K. Weekly check-ins for the first 60 days. Assign yourself as their primary contact. If you lose one, close an 11th. |
| Founder burnout (sales + engineering + support × 10 shops) | This is the real risk. By Month 4, hire implementation help. Protect 2 days/week for engineering. |
