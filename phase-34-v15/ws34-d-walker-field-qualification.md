## §4. Nadia's Qualification Call (2026-08-26)

**Call date:** 2026-08-26, 14:00 MST (20:00 UTC)
**Duration:** 52 minutes
**Participants:** Nadia Solis (Athelon), Paul Kaminski (Walker Field Aviation)

Nadia did not open with a demo. She opened with the question Marcus had recommended: *"Tell me how you're currently tracking the PT6A-114A ALS requirements on your Caravans."*

Paul's answer was direct. He'd been running a spreadsheet since he took over as DOM seven years ago. It tracked part numbers, installation dates, life limits, and his own projected replacement dates. He updated it manually after every significant maintenance event. He'd never missed an ALS item, he said, but he'd come close twice — once when the Caravan came in for an unscheduled return and the ALS sheet hadn't been updated from the previous event, and once when a replacement part was installed under a different P/N than what was in the sheet and the cycle count didn't transfer correctly.

*"The second one I caught in a binder review two weeks later. But two weeks is too long to be wrong."*

Nadia: *"What did you do when you caught it?"*

Paul: *"I corrected it and then I added a manual check step to my WO closure process. But that's the problem — I'm adding checks on top of checks to compensate for the fact that the system is a spreadsheet. At some point you're just wrapping a spreadsheet in a checklist."*

The call covered:
- Paul's current FSDO audit experience (last FSDO inspection: 2025-03. No findings. Standard 145 ramp check plus records review.)
- Caravan fleet: N416AB (*same tail as Dale Renfrow's aircraft — not uncommon for charter operators at KGJT to use sequential N-series registrations under fleet management arrangements*) and a second Caravan, N416TE. Both PT6A-114A.
- Piston fleet: 6 aircraft (4 C172S, 1 PA-28-181, 1 PA-32-301FT). Mix of private owner maintenance and local flight school contracted maintenance.
- Decision timeline: Paul was not in crisis. His ALS compliance was current. But he'd watched Dale at RMTS use the platform for five months and had asked him two questions after the WO-RMTS-003 fuel selector valve closure: *"Did it actually catch something real?"* and *"Was it worth the time to learn it?"* Dale's answers: yes and yes.
- Pricing: Nadia explained the subscription model. Paul's response: *"If it does what Dale says it does, the subscription pays for itself on one avoided discrepancy."*

At 45 minutes, Nadia asked: *"Is there anything that would prevent you from moving forward?"*

Paul: *"Honestly? No. I'm not evaluating anyone else. Dale wouldn't recommend something that didn't work. The only question is when."*

Nadia: *"When would you want to be live?"*

Paul: *"Before the November Caravan check. That's a 200-hour on N416AB, probably first week of November. I'd want the ALS boards built before that so I'm not doing the audit after the fact."*

Nadia noted the date. November 1 target for N416AB ALS boards live.
